Macro: base case, risks, positioning

  • Growth/Inflation/Rates
    • Soft-landing base case holds: Q3 growth robust, Q4 moderating but positive; unemployment drifting into the mid‑4s; inflation trending in the upper‑2s to low‑3s.
    • Policy path: High confidence in a cut today and another into December; odds favor a cut again by January. The front end should lead, with the long end biased lower into year-end.
    • Implication: Add duration (prefer intermediate-to-long Treasuries), consider curve steepeners. Credit supported; avoid reaching for lower‑quality HY as bankruptcies grind higher.
  • Fiscal/Shutdown
    • Shutdown looks prolonged (30+ days). Expect intermittent data gaps, airport delays, and benefit timing issues (SNAP). Consumer staples/discounters see a near‑term volume tailwind; travel/airlines headwinds build with service disruptions.
    • Treasury supply remains heavy; debt surpassing $38T this year is a near‑certainty. Despite issuance, easier Fed and slower growth argue for lower yields into year‑end.
  • Trade/Tariffs
    • Near‑term de‑escalation with China/South Korea is plausible (frameworks/targeted relief), but a structurally higher tariff regime into 2026 remains the central case (China mid‑20s to 30s aggregate rate).
    • Implication: Mildly inflationary, supportive of onshoring themes and select industrials; negative for import‑heavy retailers and parts of autos/electronics. Watch USD/CNY and Asia FX for tightening financial conditions spillovers.
  • Commodities/FX
    • Oil: Skewed to a $65–$75 range absent a major Middle East shock; new OPEC cuts this year look unlikely. Prefer midstream over upstream beta.
    • Natural gas: Wide volatility band with upside spikes plausible (>4 handle); utilities and chemicals should hedge exposures.
    • Gold: Strong upside skew into 2025 year‑end on deficits, geopolitics, and rate cuts; accumulation on dips remains favored.
    • USD: Downside bias as the Fed eases and Europe stabilizes; watch French political noise and added EU Russia‑sanctions for EUR volatility.
  • Geopolitics
    • Middle East: Ceasefire headline risk persists; broader regional conflict not base case near‑term. Normalization steps in the region later in the cycle are plausible and supportive for defense.
    • Russia–Ukraine: Ceasefire this year unlikely; probability rises into 2026. Additional EU sanctions on Russia remain more likely than not; occasional energy/wheat volatility spikes.

Sectors: tilts and second‑order effects

  • Rates‑sensitives/Housing
    • Falling mortgage rates into year‑end (a move below 6% looks attainable) supports homebuilders and select REITs; NYC rent growth remains firm while tech hubs see modest improvement.
  • Defense/Aerospace
    • Budget visibility improves with elevated global risk; software‑led gov‑tech (data/AI) should outgrow peers. For aero, keep a quality bias; Boeing execution risk remains elevated on 777X schedule/charges.
  • Energy
    • Favor pipelines/midstream distributions and integrateds with downstream leverage; underweight high‑beta E&Ps given range‑bound crude and potential demand softness; prepare for gas price spikes with hedges.
  • Healthcare
    • GLP‑1 price pressure risk is rising but likely incremental, not catastrophic. Managed care aided if ACA subsidy enhancements extend (more likely than not); PBMs remain in the regulatory crosshairs.
  • Internet/Media
    • TikTok divestiture path this year is more likely than not; Oracle is a leading acquirer candidate. If the asset enters transition, U.S. ad platforms can see share tailwinds; if stabilized under a compliant buyer, competitive status quo persists, but regulatory overhang eases.
  • Antitrust/Platforms
    • Live events, Apple App Store, and Amazon retail face sustained legal overhangs; remedies are likelier medium‑term than imminent but warrant modest multiple compression.
  • EVs/Clean energy
    • EV share above 30% by 2030 is a reasonable base case; U.S. climate targets for 2030 look out of reach. Favor grid infrastructure, utility‑scale storage, and power‑market software over vehicle OEM beta.
