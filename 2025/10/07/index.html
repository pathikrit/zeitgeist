Daily Memo (07-Oct-2025)

Key News Highlights

  • US Government Shutdown: Federal services and air travel are impacted, with air traffic control pressed and delays worsening. Some airport control towers shut down; federal workers brace for further disruption.
  • Middle East Tensions: Israel-Gaza war enters third year; peace talks ongoing but prospects fragile. No major leadership changes expected in Israel or Russia.
  • US Political Instability: Trump signals potential use of Insurrection Act; National Guard deployed in some cities. Legal pushback from states (notably Illinois, Oregon) continues.
  • Monetary Policy/Inflation: Inflation expectations remain elevated for 2025. Fed highly unlikely to hike, multiple cuts expected in 2025. No emergency rate cuts anticipated.
  • Labor/Jobs: Unemployment is set to rise moderately; job growth likely slower but not contracting.
  • Tech & AI: AMD stock surges on OpenAI partnership. Japan equities hitting new records as rate hike bets unwind globally. Competition in generative AI continues but status quo among leading US and China players expected.
  • Commodities & Renewables: US and China driving a sharp downward revision in renewables outlook. Renewables deployment hurt by policy and auction reforms.
  • Crypto: Bitcoin and Ethereum stability expected in upper ranges; minor chances of sharp drops. Stablecoin systemic risk from Tether/USDT seen as remote.
  • France Political Crisis: Elevates uncertainty for EU, though no immediate economic contagion risk.

Macro & Policy Environment

Growth & Labor

  • Growth Outlook: US GDP growth remains solidly positive into Q3 2025, likely in the 2–3% range. Risks of recession appear diminished near-term.
  • Unemployment/JOBs: The labor market is gradually cooling, with unemployment drifting into the low-to-mid 4% range. Authentic labor market weakness is not anticipated, but jobs growth will continue to slow.

Inflation & Rates

  • Inflation Expectations: Headline and core inflation are both set to remain persistently above the 3% mark through October 2025. The chance of inflation accelerating above 4% remains low but non-negligible.
  • Rates Trajectory: The Federal Reserve is expected to hold rates steady—rate hikes are exceptionally unlikely. The base case is for 2–3 cuts in 2025, not a “rates to zero” scenario.

Investment Takeaways

  • Persistent slightly-elevated inflation (+sticky core) implies continued real rate sensitivity in longer-duration fixed income and TIPS. Nominals will likely adjust down but with a moderate curve steepening risk if cuts proceed faster than expected.
  • Growth/tech/AI equities still favored vs. defensive sectors given GDP resilience and no signals for an imminent recession.
  • Cautious optimism toward cyclicals (consumer, industrials) as soft-landing consensus prevails.

US Fiscal/Political

  • Government Shutdown: Risk of operational and confidence shocks if the shutdown continues. Air travel, public services, and possibly healthcare could face further disruptions.
  • Domestic Politics: Intensified polarization remains a source of headline volatility. Possible use of federal force in cities could rattle risk assets but unlikely to derail core macro settings.

Sector Implications

Technology & AI

  • Semiconductors/AI: AMD surges on new OpenAI deal—underscores continued capital/exponential demand for leading-edge chip designers and infrastructure. Watch for spillover to Nvidia, Arm, and possibly smaller AI-exposed plays.
  • AI Leadership: No near-term disruption among top US/China AI model providers. Status quo persists—OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, Alibaba et al. unlikely to leapfrog each other drastically, so continued incremental competitive behavior expected.

Crypto/Blockchain

  • Bitcoin/Ethereum: Both assets are expected to hold or gently appreciate from current levels, with downside risks looking modest in the next 12 months. Massive upside to six-figure BTC is not the base case. Systemic risks from stablecoins (Tether, USDT) remain subdued for now.
  • Market Sentiment: Crypto volatility to be driven more by macro/monetary than systemic shocks; only extreme tail events threaten current ecosystem stability.

Renewables & Commodities

  • Clean Energy: The US and China policy environments are now pressuring renewable buildout outlooks. Watch for further capex/earnings pressure on solar and wind equities.
  • Commodities/Energy: Slower renewables progress may backstop traditional energy (oil, natural gas) and supporting infrastructure in the near/medium term.

Geopolitics

  • Middle East: Israel-Gaza conflict remains unresolved. Ceasefire prospects are slim; risk premium may persist, particularly if global energy supply routes are threatened—even as markets become more desensitized.
  • Russia/Ukraine: No expectation of significant leadership change in Russia, and no anticipated ceasefire in the near term—continuing to freeze Europe's risk outlook.
  • France/French Markets: Macron under mounting pressure; expect French assets to underperform until resolution, but not yet a continent-wide issue.

Individual Names & Themes

  • AMD: Significant outperformer from OpenAI partnership; further rerating possible as Nvidia-esque growth narrative picks up.
  • Meta/Apple: No significant surprises from AI model performance; iterative upgrades to devices/OS remain the focus.
  • Renewable Energy Firms: Sentiment weakens as policy and cost headwinds mount. Short-term caution warranted.
  • Airlines/Travel: Short-term disruptions from the shutdown may dent outlook and bookings, while labor costs remain sticky.

Concluding Bias

  • Overall, markets remain growth-bullish with mild inflation overhang and “Fed support” scenario in the baseline.
  • Remain overweight sectors/tickers benefiting from sticky growth and AI-driven capex, underweight rate-sensitive duration or pure-play renewables until policy uncertainty clears.
  • Maintain a close watch on any escalation from US political or global geopolitical flashpoints, especially if labor (strike risk), energy, or fiscal shocks broaden.
