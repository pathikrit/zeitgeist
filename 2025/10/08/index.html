Macro Outlook

Growth, Inflation, and Fed Policy

  • Baseline US GDP growth expectations for 2025 cluster in the 2–2.5% range, with minimal risk seen for either outsized expansion or outright recession.
  • Unemployment is expected to trend just above 4.2%, suggesting a slightly softening but still resilient labor market—modest pressure on wage inflation likely to persist.
  • Headline and core inflation are expected to remain entrenched above 3%, with limited probability of a surge above 4%. Wage growth is anticipated to stay ahead of inflation on average, supporting consumer discretionary sectors.
  • Multiple Fed rate cuts are implied as the base case for the year (three cuts most likely), with near-zero probability given to rate hikes or emergency cuts. This supports a constructive outlook for risk assets, with incremental tailwinds for both equities and long-duration bonds.
  • Government spending is likely to increase further in 2025, compounding fiscal deficit pressures. Total US debt is all but certain to be above $38T by year-end.
  • Macroeconomic policy risk from government shutdowns and looming political confrontations (debt ceiling, budget) remain acute short-term volatility drivers.

2nd/3rd Order Effects:

  • Persistent high government spending may keep upward pressure on yields and inflation expectations, while central bank dovishness may blunt the effects on risk assets.
  • Continued wage growth over inflation could underpin consumer spending resilience, especially in tech/consumer discretionary.
  • Gold's rally signals hedging against both inflation and geopolitical instability. Momentum flows into gold and related assets may further increase if macro or political risk escalates.

Thematic & Sector Takeaways

Equities & US Markets

  • S&P 500 and major US indices are expected to preserve a strong upward bias, with high confidence in new yearly highs and end-2025 levels solidly above 6,500. Risk of a major drawdown appears low barring exogenous shocks.
  • Bullion moves, higher digital asset prices, and robust ETF inflows suggest ongoing strong demand for both risk and inflation-hedged assets.
  • Recession fears have faded, and the consensus is for stable expansion, supporting growth-at-a-reasonable-price and secular tech leadership themes.

Energy & Climate

  • The probability of reaching global 2°C warming before 2050 is increasingly accepted, with energy consumption still led by oil and coal through 2030, and ongoing hurdles for large-scale renewables adoption.
  • US and EU are unlikely to meet medium-term climate goals, supporting modest renewable energy buildout (notably solar in the US), but with oil and coal entrenched globally.
  • Natural gas pricing risk is skewed higher, with a strong base of $3+ expected in 2025.
  • Expansion of nuclear for data/tech infrastructure (US military bases) is increasingly likely; second-order ramifications for utility, infrastructure, and tech hardware suppliers.

Technology & Innovation

  • AI remains the central theme, both as a driver of tech stock outperformance and as a regulatory/policy risk—OpenAI is favored to IPO sooner than peers; Google, Nvidia, and other AI leaders remain in focus for outsized returns and headline risk.
  • Semiconductor production, especially US-based, is projected for double-digit growth, reinforcing the structural bull case for supply chain and hardware names.
  • Anticipate ongoing debate over privacy, regulation, and product updates (especially from Google/Alphabet).

Consumer & Healthcare

  • Consumer spending remains robust, as evidenced by high engagement in seasonal retail events and sustained earnings power.
  • In healthcare, a new Type 1 diabetes cure is seen as an outside chance before 2033; near-term sector focus is likely to remain on regular pharma innovation, weight loss drugs, and regulatory trends.

Crypto & Digital Assets

  • Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana are all expected to make significant new highs in 2025, though runaway targets (e.g., BTC $1M) are not in view.
  • Downside for major cryptos is seen as limited for the coming year, with strong risk-on sentiment. Crypto flows may further benefit from any flare-up in inflation, financial repression, or geopolitical volatility.

Country & Geopolitical Watch

  • Highly public DOJ and National Guard activity, plus mention of potential Insurrection Act use, keep domestic stability in focus. Shutdown politics may drive additional volatility in rate markets and government-linked sectors.
  • Little expectation of a US recession, but fiscal/funding surprises could shift the macro narrative quickly.

Global Political Landscape

  • No imminent threat seen in EU member exits, though continued regional risk could fuel further bouts of volatility or safe haven flows.
  • China’s economic catch-up to the US remains unlikely by 2030. Market attention likely to stay on high-profile events (trade deals, Taiwan risk) rather than regime change.
  • Israel-Gaza conflict and related geopolitical volatility may buoy commodity prices, notably energy.
