Daily Memo (24-Jul-2025)

  • Epstein Files and Political Fallout: Trump’s name confirmed in newly released Epstein files, sparking partisan conflict. DOJ and Congressional inquiries intensify; subpoenas expanded to Clinton circle.
  • Climate Regulation Unwinding: Trump EPA moves to repeal key climate findings, potentially weakening environmental regulation and enabling fossil fuel sector gains.
  • Tesla Earnings Warnings: Musk signals a "rough patch" after weak sales; new affordable EV model teased, but confidence remains shaky. Shares down, automation/AI, tariffs, and regulatory issues remain at fore.
  • AI Policy Push: Trump administration launches AI policy plan, denouncing “woke” AI. Nvidia and AMD CEOs publicly supportive. Regulatory, copyright, and privacy themes gain federal attention.
  • US-Japan Auto Trade Shifts: New trade deal may favor Japanese automakers, putting US competitors at a disadvantage; further clouding tariff policy and competitive outlook for US auto sector.
  • Thailand-Cambodia Border Crisis: Escalating military conflict increases regional instability; focus on potential supply disruptions (esp. rare earths, agriculture).
  • Federal Reserve Under Fire: Trump publicly criticizes Powell and Fed spending, plans high-profile visit; potential to raise volatility in rates/yields.

Macro & Markets

US Economic Outlook

  • Growth & Risk: Odds strongly support the US avoiding recession this year (and into 2025). However, the consensus is for slow/moderate GDP growth (~1.5-2%); robust growth (“Trump boom”) is considered plausible but not dominant.
  • Labor Market: Unemployment is projected to drift higher, but odds of a severe spike (e.g., over 9%) remain modest. Expect some weakness reflected in higher unemployment but not an acute jobs crisis in the near term.
  • Inflation: Headline and core CPI are both likely to stay in the upper-2% to mid-3% range into late summer and possibly above 3% into 2025. US inflation, while not runaway, is resistant to rapid normalization.
  • Monetary Policy: Multiple Fed cuts this year are still more likely than not, but the outlook for aggressive easing has diminished. Expect 1-2 cuts at most, conditional on inflation easing; no sign of a return to ZIRP.
  • Debt & Fiscal Policy: US national debt will pass $38 trillion by year-end with very high likelihood. This, combined with sticky inflation and elevated yields (10Y seen above 4.6%), signals continued supply-driven pressure on Treasuries and upward risk to risk-free rates.

2nd/3rd Order Effects

  • Inflation pressure + higher-for-longer yields could support continued leadership by real asset sectors (commodities, energy, real estate with pricing power).
  • Fiscal expansion and higher rates may begin to pressure housing, leveraged consumers, small-cap equities, and unprofitable tech in due course.

Geopolitics & Policy

  • Ukraine / Russia: Little expectation of ceasefire or major regime change; risk premium remains for energy/agriculture, but tail event of sudden détente is discounted.
  • Middle East / Iran: Leadership and regional balance changes unlikely in immediate term; energy/defense positioning more stable but vigilant for shocks.
  • Climate & Regulation: US regulatory climate facing potential reversal, especially around emissions and automotive rules. Factors to watch: carbon sector stocks, auto efficiency trade.
  • Asia: Thailand-Cambodia skirmish underlines Southeast Asia’s geopolitical fragility; possible supply risks for select mineral/agriculture/tech value chains.
  • China/Europe: Trade negotiations remain tense, with rare earths and climate commitments in focus.

2nd/3rd Order Effects

  • US regulatory retrenchment on climate could drive underweighting of “clean tech” and ESG-thematic stocks, benefit traditional energy/utilities and auto upstarts that can exploit new loopholes.
  • Ongoing US-China/EU friction to benefit alternate sourcing countries (e.g., Vietnam, India) and support select US/EU industrials.

Sector Strategy

Tech & AI

  • Mega-cap Tech Domination: Nvidia is widely expected to be the world’s largest public company by year-end, with Microsoft trailing. AI and semiconductor momentum to persist. Software (especially cloud, AI-focused) and leading hardware names to retain premium multiples.
  • Regulatory/Antitrust: Emerging risk for major platforms (Meta, Alphabet), but odds of forced breakups or major structural penalties this year are minimal.
  • AI Product Cycle: Major new model (GPT-5) release is highly likely this year. Key sub-themes: AI infrastructure, cloud spend, and hardware (Nvidia, AMD, Amazon AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud).
  • Big Tech Earnings: Expect recurring focus on AI/Gen AI, cloud, subscription models, and operating margin enhancement. Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Apple, Microsoft, and Tesla to remain AI narrative beneficiaries, barring idiosyncratic regulatory or product failures.
  • Tesla: Key “earnings call” topics include autonomy, AI software/robotics (Dojo, Optimus, Robotaxi), tariffs, new affordable EVs, and regulatory maneuvering. Short-term volatility expected due to execution and demand questions.
  • Apple: Expected to lean into wearable, AI, China manufacturing, and service revenues. No near-term breakthrough hardware cycle (e.g., no foldable iPhone).

2nd/3rd Order Effects

  • Persistent narrow leadership (“Magnificent 7” share remains high in S&P 500), with limited market breadth.
  • AI regulation/safety bills not seen as a near-term headwind; watch for possible catalyst if enacted.
  • Semiconductor cyclicality still a risk; supply chain shocks from Asia/EM always a background concern.

Energy, Commodities, Crypto

  • Energy: Ongoing geopolitical friction (Russia/Ukraine, Middle East) maintains risk premium, but energy rallies likely to be moderate unless escalation occurs.
  • Commodities: Sticky inflation and real asset demand (gold, metals, rare earths) supported by fiscal expansion and inconsistent supply chains.
  • Crypto: Bitcoin and Ethereum after a volatile 2024-25, with base case further upside for both through year-end (BTC $130K seen plausible; ETH to new highs slightly favored, Cardano ETF likely approved). Lower but non-negligible downside tail risk for “crypto winter.”

2nd/3rd Order Effects

  • Blockchain-based US Treasury transactions could provide fintech tailwinds but not a near-term market mover.
  • Outperformance opportunity may shift from BTC/ETH toward “real economy-linked” assets (energy, ag, gold) if CPI/Fed path turns more hawkish.

Autos & Industrials

  • Autos: US-Japan trade deal, plus relaxed climate policy, likely benefit foreign automakers and traditional US OEMs. Tesla faces visibility gap as it transitions to affordable EVs amidst regulatory and competitive pressure.
  • Industrials: SpaceX commercial launches remain robust, underscoring leadership in new space sector.

Individual Names To Watch

  • Nvidia (NVDA): Retains AI/semiconductor leadership narrative; growth focus on gaming, automotive, sovereign/robotic themes. Most likely to maintain top market cap slot. Sensitive to GPU/AI infra spending, possible China shocks.
  • Tesla (TSLA): Near-term bearish on “rough patch” warning and sales worry, watch for execution of “affordable model,” and regulatory/AI headlines (especially tariffs and automation).
  • Apple (AAPL): Growth drivers in wearables, services, stable product cycle; watch for margin effect from tariffs/China supply chain.
  • Microsoft (MSFT): Cloud, AI/collaboration (Copilot, Azure, Teams, OpenAI) continue to drive performance. M&A/Activision integration minor headline risk.
  • Amazon (AMZN): AI, AWS, Prime membership content remain themes; supply chain, tariff, and margin focus.
  • Alphabet (GOOG): Key focus on AI (Gemini/YouTube Shorts/Cloud), antitrust litigation, and data center scaling.

Risk Monitor

  • Tail Risks:

    • Major geoevent (meteor strike, nuclear detonation) not central base case but justification for hedges (volatility, gold, defense, selected tail-risk insurance).
    • Marketwide circuit breaker risk present but not dominant.
    • US debt ceiling/default risk is extremely muted for this year.

  • Global Policy: Monitor for rapid US fiscal or regulatory pivots, new international sanctions or regime changes, and surprise in Asian markets.

Summary Investment Lean

  • Stay overweight in US mega-cap tech/AI leaders, with selective tilt into quality energy/commodity plays and real asset hedges.
  • Retain strategic exposure to core crypto (BTC, ETH), adding/lowering on structural volatility.
  • Watch for rotation into traditional sectors (energy, autos, select industrials) if US policy unwinds on climate or tariffs.
  • Maintain risks controls for high-volatility events and idiosyncratic geopolitical shocks, but no urgent need for broad de-risking.
  • Monitor inflation/rate shock risk for late-cycle impact on consumer and small/mid-cap stocks.

Note: Remain attentive to evolving regulatory themes (AI, trade, climate), Fed policy signals, and headline risks affecting major individual names and sectors.
