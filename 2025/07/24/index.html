Macroeconomic & Policy Outlook

US Macro/Market Cycle

  • The market anticipates modest but positive US GDP growth for 2025. Odds are highest for sub-2.5% real GDP prints, with stronger numbers seen as quite unlikely. Odds of a 2025 US recession remain elevated versus recent years, but confidence overall leans against an outright contraction.
  • Wage growth is expected to run just ahead of inflation, supporting consumer demand.
  • The S&P 500 is widely expected to finish 2025 positive, with tails to the downside relatively contained.
  • Unemployment is projected to climb but remain manageable (~4–4.2% levels most likely), reflecting mild economic cooling rather than stress.

Second-Order Risks:

  • The very high S&P optimism and suppressed volatility expectations leave the market vulnerable to policy surprises, global shocks, or sudden inflation shifts.
  • A government shutdown within 2025 remains a non-trivial possibility, which could halt spending programs, weigh on Q3–Q4 growth, and add near-term yields volatility.
  • Geopolitics (Middle East, Russia/Ukraine, Asia) continue to present tail event risk.

US Fiscal and Debt

  • Total US debt is on track to surpass $38T during 2025, with annual deficit reduction seen as unlikely.
  • US Treasury yields are expected to remain steady in the 4–4.5% range, with little market-implied risk of yield spikes.
  • Federal default risk is seen as remote but not zero—still worth hedging at the margin if political brinksmanship escalates.

Investment Implications:

  • Persistent high deficits suggest increased upward pressure on long-term bond yields over time, but the yield curve is not pricing acute stress now.
  • With budgets strained, risk of “surprise” tax or spending policy changes is heightened in event of an economic/market shock.

Fed, Inflation, and Rates

  • The Fed is broadly expected to hold rates steady for 2025, with at most one or two rate cuts. A hike is viewed as extremely unlikely barring unexpected inflation.
  • High market agreement on rates presents risk if inflation surprises to the upside, or if global shocks force emergency easing.
  • Fed communications will emphasize dual mandates, spending, and tariffs—watch for policy inflections post-election.

Second-Order/Asset Flows:

  • With limited rate volatility expected, bond market positioning will focus more on inflation break-evens and credit risk/policy signals than dramatic duration moves.

Trade & Tariffs

  • Trump’s first-year tariff push is near-certain; China and semiconductors are clear targets. Tariffs on copper and even pharma have a strong chance of implementation.
  • Probability of US exiting WTO or new forced trade shocks is low but rising.
  • Resulting supply chain cost pressures could stoke sector-specific US inflation and shift capital expenditure back to onshoring themes.

Key Sector & Thematic Takeaways

Tech & Semiconductors

  • Robust US semiconductor production growth expected in 2025 (mid-high single-digit percent/ramp), despite looming tariffs on both chips and raw inputs.
  • Tech is a market leadership sector (Alphabet earnings strength just reaffirmed this), but regulatory risk remains – Trump’s AI order and occasional policy threats to social media and hardware manufacturers could add headline risk.
  • Large cap platforms look resilient, but small caps could see higher volatility as focus on AI “fairness” and “wokeness” policies brings uncertainty.

Energy & Commodities

  • Oil complex signals pronounced volatility: spot WTI likely to fluctuate broadly, with current bias toward mild downside. Low-$60s/bbl possible by year-end, but spikes toward $80/bbl (if OPEC or geopolitics flare up) remain plausible.
  • Gasoline prices in states like Texas and California may face periodic spikes ($3+ and $5+, respectively), but national averages should be manageable for the consumer.
  • Renewables: US and EU are unlikely to meet 2030 climate/decarbonization targets, reinforcing incremental investment over radical transformation in energy/utility sectors. Solar and Tesla’s energy business should see sustained but not explosive growth; expect targets to be revised, not met.

Financials

  • JPMorgan CEO succession attracts attention; continuity expected with internal candidates. No acute destabilization risk.
  • UnitedHealth’s federal probe clouds the healthcare insurance space. Be alert to competitors’ legal exposure and DOJ’s shifting focus.

Healthcare & Pharma

  • Healthcare costs/features are in political crosshairs: movement to repeal/alter ACA is non-trivial, especially post-election, but not imminent.
  • Pricing power in high-profile drugs like Ozempic could ebb in coming quarters, though aggressive price collapses look unlikely.
  • Near-term FDA approvals for key names (e.g., Donidalorsen, Sebetralstat) support selective biotech allocation.

Real Estate

  • Rent inflation is projected to be persistent in major metros like San Francisco (>2%) and New York (up to mid-single digits), with SF more robust and NYC showing mixed signals.
  • Risk trend: Potential for earthquake scenarios, especially in California, calls for strategic hedges or avoidance in highly levered REITs and insurance plays.
  • Broader REIT outlook supported by steady wage/inflation growth but vulnerable if rates surprise higher.

Industrials & Materials

  • Tariffs on copper/metals are highly likely, which may lift input volatility for US manufacturers and spur more near-term inflation in materials-heavy sectors.
  • Defense spending and energy infrastructure are not flagged for cuts, and could see cyclical benefit in the event of further global turmoil.

EVs & Autos

  • Tesla outlook muted: weak sales, price discounting, and fading EV demand identified in news and markets; rapid expansion through new affordable models or energy milestones not anticipated in 2025–2026.
  • ICE/hybrid launches by Tesla are highly unlikely, suggesting sector pressure if EV malaise persists. EV supply chain and battery plays require more scrutiny for margin compression and competitive threats.

Noteworthy Individual Names

  • Tesla: Facing multiple headwinds – weak Q2, limited product/driven upside short-term, and broader EV demand fatigue.
  • Alphabet/Google: Driving tech/market sentiment up; earnings leadership persists.
  • UnitedHealth: Under DOJ investigation; material headline and litigation risk for entire managed care space.
  • JPMorgan: CEO transition viewed as stable; no adverse succession shock expected.

Potential Geopolitical & Black Swan Watchlist

  • Escalating Middle East/Gaza humanitarian disaster poses headline oil/geopolitical risk.
  • Renewed Russia–Ukraine drone attacks maintain global supply chain and risk premium.
  • Thailand–Cambodia border crisis could trigger EM risk tightening.
  • Ghislaine Maxwell/Jeffrey Epstein dossier drama fuels unpredictable US political volatility, possible regulatory pivots.

Summary/Actionable View

Maintain a pro-risk stance, but manage exposures:

  • Keep allocations to tech/semis strong but hedged for tariff shocks and regulatory volatility.
  • Healthcare and REITs are at risk from regulatory and headline shocks—watch for entry points if sector overreacts.
  • Favor US-centric supply chains and industrials that can benefit from domestic capex and onshoring.
  • Monitor fiscal/bond headlines and have liquidity/cash alternatives for political standoffs or debt ceiling noise.
  • Maintain inflation hedges, but expect slow downward drift barring new exogenous shocks.
  • Watch for idiosyncratic earnings/DOE/FDA events for high-quality single-name opportunities.

Remain nimble and watch headlines for sudden policy/geopolitical dislocations that could quickly alter sector outlooks.
