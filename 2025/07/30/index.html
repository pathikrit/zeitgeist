Macro Themes & Second-Order Effects

1. Global Trade Disruption & US-India Relations

  • Trump’s new tariffs against India, plus extra penalties tied to India’s Russia commerce, will almost certainly escalate trade tensions and could trigger targeted retaliation.
  • Near-term effects: Higher import costs on select goods, pressure on Indian equities, and renewed supply chain risks for US corporates exposed to India.
  • Longer-run: Accelerated push for supply chain diversification out of India, possible uptick in deglobalization rhetoric, pressure on tech and pharma outsourcing, and likely knock-on impact on companies reliant on Indian engineering/equity markets.

2. Strong GDP Headline Masks Economic Divergence

  • Recent GDP growth remains robust, but underlying weakness, especially in consumer and investment segments, could mean:
    • Rally in risk assets may be fragile; potential for disappointment if incoming data worsens.
    • Policy signals: Fed hesitancy to cut rates (internal dissent emerging) reflects uncertainty; financials and rate-sensitive sectors may remain volatile.
    • Safe-haven assets (e.g., gold, TIPS) may benefit if cracks widen.

3. Geopolitical Risks: Iran and Regime Stability

  • No imminent change expected in Iranian leadership; talk of regime risk persists but events remain distant outliers.
  • Ongoing regional volatility (Gaza, Thailand-Cambodia) and the West’s shifting stance on Palestine may keep defense and energy risk premia elevated.
