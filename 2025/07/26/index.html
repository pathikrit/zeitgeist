Daily Memo (26-Jul-2025)

Top News Highlights

  • Geopolitics: Trump calls for Israel to escalate action against Hamas; France recognizes Palestinian state, straining transatlantic relations.
  • US Macro: S&P 500 hits 5th straight record; Trump administration releasing $6B in education funds; continued heat wave affecting over 100M Americans; Trump escalates rhetoric against the Fed.
  • Corporate Action: Skydance/Paramount merger closing in August following concessions; ongoing cyberattack concerns at Microsoft with implications for cloud and enterprise security sectors.
  • International: An uptick in Southeast Asian tensions (Thai-Cambodian conflict), issues involving energy flows in Europe remain in the background.
  • Health: EU approves Gilead’s twice-yearly injectable HIV drug (potential sector tailwind); first US West Nile virus death of the year; lingering global pandemic vigilance.
  • Tech: Apple launches iOS 26 with consumer lock-in features; Samsung and Google announce new hardware and software rollouts; Microsoft under scrutiny for cyber exposures.

Macro & Policy Backdrop

US Economy and Market Tone

  • Soft Landing Persists: Broad consensus expects a continued ‘soft landing’ macro backdrop—low recession risk short term, with positive earnings growth and mild unemployment increases. Recession risk through year-end foregrounded but not acute.
    • S&P 500 highly likely to remain positive for the year, though further steep price appreciation looks less probable from here.
  • Labor Market: Unemployment expected to edge a bit higher in coming months but dramatic job losses are not imminent. Tech sector layoffs are expected to be steeper y/y, albeit likely offset by other sectors.
  • Inflation & Rates: Both core and headline inflation are likely to remain above pre-pandemic levels but moderate compared to 2022-24 peaks. Wage growth is expected to outpace inflation, supporting consumer spending but putting a floor under CPI.
    • Energy and commodity price volatility may drive short-term CPI spikes, especially if broad-based tariffs are enacted.
  • US Debt & Fiscal Policy: National debt continues its sharp march upwards; debt ceiling will be raised. Expects renewed focus on long-term fiscal imbalances (debt-to-GDP likely > 120% in the next cycle); this adds upward bias to yields, especially on the long end.

Trade, Tariffs & Geopolitics

  • New Tariff Regime Imminent: Major new US tariffs on a wide set of trading partners (China, Mexico, South Korea, Taiwan, EU, etc.) are highly likely in 2025, including on critical supply chain inputs (copper, possibly semiconductors, and even Canadian goods).
    • Immediate impacts: Cost-push inflation, upward pressure on finished goods and autos, supply chain disruptions, retaliatory trade actions likely from Asia and the EU.
  • Middle East & EU Instability: Renewed conflict in Gaza, France’s recognition of Palestine increase headline risk for energy prices and defense-related equities, but risk of direct global market spillover is limited for now.

Energy & Commodities

  • Oil & Gas Outlook: Oil prices likely to remain in a lower range barring large supply shocks, even as OPEC cut risk lingers. US natural gas expected higher y/y, supporting earnings for gas-exposed producers, while coal remains a default global baseload despite policy rhetoric.
  • Energy Mix: No immediate regime shift to renewables—coal remains (unfortunately for carbon risk) the largest global source through 2030.

Sectors

Technology & Semiconductors

  • Chips: US semiconductor output surging in 2025, but new tariffs on Asian chip supply chains could undermine the pace of margin expansion and disrupt tech firms with complex global sourcing.
  • Tech Layoffs: Big tech continuing to rightsize workforces; expect margin efficiency focus and potential near-term operating leverage.
  • Cybersecurity: Microsoft and other cloud providers face rising risk and cost of cyber breaches—potential negative second-order impact on sector trust and EOQ sales pipelines.
  • Big Tech Antitrust: Antitrust risk is materializing for Amazon, Apple, and Live Nation. Over a multi-year horizon, expect headline-driven volatility and incremental regulatory headwinds for dominant platforms.
  • Leadership Shifts: NVIDIA likely to retain its position as world’s largest company by market cap into year-end; further CEO transitions (JPMorgan, Google, Amazon, OpenAI) are low-probability tail events for 2025.

Energy, Utilities, Materials

  • Utilities and Clean Tech: US solar buildout continues at pace, but headline-driven volatility can be expected—tariffs or commodity price shifts could create buying opportunities on weakness.
  • Commodities: Copper and other industrial metals face new supply risks via tariffs, supporting prices but also increasing input costs for downstream US manufacturing and renewables; hedge cost structure in exposed sectors.

Financials & Real Assets

  • Financial Sector: Bank sector leadership (JPMorgan) transition likely in near future, but systemic risk is muted. Watch for US market circuit breaker events as a tail risk (not base case).
  • Property, REITs, Insurance: No new evidence of forced shocks, but earthquake risk in California and Japan is non-trivial over the longer horizon. Margin headwinds for property & casualty insurers with tail risk events possible; REITs remain interest-rate sensitive in rising Treasury yield context.

Healthcare & Pharma

  • Pharma Tariffs Watch: While less imminent than in tech or autos, watch for health sector tariff action—it would be inflationary for drug/biotech supply chains but not yet central scenario.
  • Drug Approvals: EU advances in HIV drug technology could spill over to US, supporting sectoral outperformance for leading developers (Gilead, others).

Asset Class & Strategy Takeaways

  • Equities: Remain overweight US broad indices with a tilt toward quality and margin-levered growth; expect only moderate further price upside near term, but positive earnings momentum should persist.
  • Bonds: Upward pressure on yields through year-end due to fiscal slippage and tariff-driven inflation; long duration and TIPS fair for tactical hedging, but limited appreciation likely.
  • Commodities: Remain cautious on oil upside unless fresh global supply disruption emerges. Copper and other critical inputs (rare earths, lithium) supported by trade policy but likely volatile.
  • Crypto: Strong odds for continued upward momentum in Bitcoin and Ethereum, supported by ETF flows and macro uncertainty. Watch for sudden volatility amid broader market stress; base case for new highs by year-end.

Company-Specific Notes

  • Nvidia expected to maintain global leadership by market cap; semis broadly supported by strong US production but supply chains exposed to tariffs.
  • Apple and Amazon both face increasing antitrust and regulatory scrutiny; expect episodic volatility but sector weight remains dominant.
  • Gilead positioned for upside in HIV prevention market after EU approval.
  • Microsoft vulnerable to ongoing cyber risk headlines; monitor for sector-wide repercussions or guidance revision.
  • Live Nation faces high antitrust risk; monitor for potential forced divestiture or margin compression.

Risks to Watch

  • Acceleration of US/China/EU trade wars beyond priced-in scenarios—watch for escalation signals or sectoral retaliation.
  • Outlier macro shocks: Geopolitical surprise (Middle East, Asia), major natural disaster, or a new pandemic, while low-probability, could change cross-asset relationships rapidly.
  • Systemic financial risk remains a tail risk, not the base case.
