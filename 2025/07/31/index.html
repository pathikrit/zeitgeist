Macro and Policy

Fiscal & Monetary Landscape

  • Spending & Deficit: Federal spending cuts of any meaningful scale appear extremely unlikely in the coming quarters. Fiscal tailwinds continue supporting equities and risk assets; deficit will remain elevated and government stimulus persistent in the FY25 outlook.
  • Interest Rates: Fed is currently on hold, with base case anticipating up to two cuts in 2025, but a non-trivial (~1 in 4) chance of no cuts as long as strong GDP and labor data persist. Emergency cuts and stagflation are not central risks.
  • Trade/Tariffs: Sudden 15% tariffs on Korean imports underscore policy unpredictability in trade, likely to drive cyclical volatility in auto, electronics, and industrial supply chains.

Positioning Implications: Maintain exposure to risk-assets and sectors benefiting from fiscal support and policy continuity. Remain alert for volatility flashpoints linked to tariffs and new trade disputes, particularly for multinational manufacturers.

Geopolitical Risk

  • Middle East: Growing Western and Arab momentum for a two-state solution places additional tail risk on regional stability, but immediate energy supply disruptions unlikely without kinetic escalation.
  • Russia/Ukraine: Low odds of a near-term ceasefire, and even lower probability for leadership change in Russia. Ukraine NATO accession is very unlikely next year—status quo continues.
  • East Asia: Limited risk of a China-Taiwan military clash in the very near term, but tensions justify defensive posturing in globally exposed manufacturing supply chains and EM-focused portfolios.
  • Iran: While succession risk for Khamenei is non-trivial, outright upheaval is not imminent. Watch for incremental oil market reaction if rumors escalate.

Sectors

Technology

  • Big Tech Earnings: Microsoft and Meta demonstrating strong operational leverage from AI rollouts. Apple's price hike plans signal confidence in premium demand; no foldables, but further refresh cycles will support margins.
  • Antitrust/Fragmentation: No short-term forced breakup for Meta, but continued regulatory pressure and M&A scrutiny remain in background.

Consumer & Healthcare

  • Cannabis/Healthcare Regulation: Moderate probability of cannabis regulatory rescheduling in 2025 supports optionality in US healthcare and consumer stocks exposed to the theme. Mainstream healthcare and pharmaceutical vaccine policy changes have low odds.
  • Consumer Sentiment: Entertainment and consumer discretionary demand steady, with no box office or product “super-cycles” on immediate horizon.

Energy, Climate, Insurance

  • Hurricanes/Natural Cat Events: Consensus expects a moderately active hurricane season. Monitor for claims exposure in insurance and real-assets/infrastructure in Gulf states.
  • Commodities/Oil: Geopolitical situation stable enough to limit oil/gas price shock in near-term, but monitor Iran, Middle East negotiations.

Crypto & Digital Assets

  • Stablecoins: Market openly fretting over USDT solvency and depeg, but actual tail risk is remote for 2025.
  • Ethereum & Altcoins: Outperformance of ETH versus legacy crypto is plausible near term; sector remains in “risk-on” mode, but event-driven dislocations still possible.

Individual Names & Corporate Themes

  • Meta: Beat-and-raise quarter with AI engagement, offsetting regulatory overhang.

Tactical Watchlist

  • Monitor for any abrupt macro or trade policy pivots; maintain defensive hedges in sectors directly exposed to tariffs.
  • Remain overweight AI/cloud tech majors on continued positive earnings revision cycle.
  • Maintain defensive positions in insurance and utility/rate-sensitive sectors given both hurricane risk and uncertain Fed cut cadence.
  • Be aware of sudden tail event risk in stablecoins and monitor for any catalysts in cannabis or healthcare re/regulation.

Overall Thesis:
The macro environment remains constructive for US risk assets, with fiscal and monetary policy supportive and no major disinflation or fiscal retrenchment on the horizon. Geopolitical developments should be watched, but short-term global disruption odds are contained. Technology sector, led by AI and digital infrastructure, remains a structural outperformer, and US consumer demand is healthy, with select opportunities in regulatory themes and digital assets.
