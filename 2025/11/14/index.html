Macro & Rates

Inflation, Fed & Employment

  • Fed Rate Path: Markets have reduced probability of imminent rate cuts following recent Fedspeak and stable inflation prints. December FOMC seen as likely pause but with generally dovish expectations into 2026; three cuts in 2025 remain consensus, though timing/pace could move with data.
  • Inflation: CPI prints suggest headline and core will remain in the 2.9-3.2% YoY window for the coming months. November CPI (11-Dec) is a critical test; further cooling implies 2026 rate cuts/softer USD.
  • Labor Market: Soft-landing thesis is holding; yet, consumer-linked retailers (WMT, TGT) emphasize SNAP, holiday and automation to shape spending and margin narratives. Unemployment risk remains skewed towards gradual rise, not spike.

Fiscal Policy & US Debt

  • Government Funding: Extension passed, but shutdown/budget gridlock risk remains into December. Another shutdown event before year end is possible, affecting sentiment and rates.
  • Deficit & Treasuries: Aversion to long-dated US debt persists with auctions requiring higher yields; large 10Y auction coming in February's refunding cycle. Macro/fiscal backdrop supports higher long-term rates even if the Fed eases later in 2026.

International Central Banks

  • ECB/BOE: ECB and BoE both expected to pause or cut in December policy meetings. EUR and GBP likely to remain under pressure vs USD as central bank tone signals.
  • BOJ (JPY): No sudden YCC shift expected in December, but JGB-UST spreads remain critical for global bond risk.

Geopolitics & Energy

Middle East Risks

  • Israel/West Bank: Settler violence and mosque arson raise regional tension. Odds of broader escalation to Lebanon or Iran remain non-negligible through year-end, especially with OPEC+ and Strait of Hormuz tensions (Iran tanker seizure).
  • Oil Market: OPEC+ Nov 30 meeting likely to preserve restrictive stance unless recession risk increases. Oil currently ranges mid-60s to high-70s into year-end.

Russia/Ukraine

  • Military & Civilian Impact: Russia ramps up Kyiv strikes; winter could bring infrastructure disruptions. EU debates aid and risk, but no sign of negotiations. Ukraine strikes into Russia or on energy assets would swing commodities further.

China

  • Taiwan Risk: Near-term Taiwan tensions stable but broader risk of military clash remains elevated into 2026; US-China diplomatic engagement remains limited.
  • US-China Trade: Select US tariff rollbacks (cf. Switzerland) signal tactical deals are possible elsewhere, but large-scale easing unlikely near term.

Financials

Real Estate & REITs
