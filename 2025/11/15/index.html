Daily Memo (15-Nov-2025)

Key News (Summarized)

  • Fed, CPI, Fiscal: November FOMC meeting set a patient tone; latest CPI indicates annualized 3.0–3.1% inflation; policy uncertainty as US government shutdown deadline nears.
  • Macro & Geopolitics: Trump administration escalates Venezuela pressure; Iran seizes a tanker in the Strait of Hormuz; China-Japan tensions over Taiwan; major Southern California storm/flood risk.
  • Policy Actions: Trump withdraws support for Marjorie Taylor Greene (signals evolving GOP alignment); Trump drops tariffs on various food items to address consumer inflation.
  • Tech & Supply Chains: Tesla, GM move to eliminate all China-made parts in American vehicles, accelerating US re-shoring and undermining parts of the Asian supply chain.
  • Ongoing Middle East Tensions: Israel continues strikes in Gaza and Lebanon; OPEC+ meeting approaches amid volatile oil markets and inflation pass-through risk.
  • AI/Tech: Google Gemini 3.0 release appears imminent, with major LLM benchmarks in view.

Macro

US Economy & Rates

  • Market is pricing in steady rates through the remainder of 2025 with a moderate chance of two rate cuts into 2026; a recession before end-2026 is not consensus, though probability for a US or global downturn has crept up.
  • CPI/PPI prints are around 3%, above the Fed's explicit target, keeping the hurdle high for aggressive policy easing.
  • Fiscal issuance remains extremely heavy; debt level to hit/exceed $39T before 2027, and auctions are key for rates and multiples (watch for higher term premium if demand falters).

Implications

  • Extended higher-for-longer risk premium persists: expensive/high-duration growth and speculative assets are vulnerable to further real yield repricing.
  • Surprise acceleration in inflation or failed Treasury auctions can reignite volatility and challenge both equities and bonds.

Global Macro/Geopolitics

  • Iran’s actions in the Strait of Hormuz and Venezuela brinkmanship keep an oil/geopolitics risk premium alive; Middle East escalation could translate to commodity spikes and strengthen defensive/dividend themes.
  • US-China (and US/EU/India) trade dynamics remain volatile: tariff changes can whiplash specific multinationals and global supply chains.
  • China-Japan friction and Taiwan-related signals are rising; secondary impacts include JPY volatility, semiconductor supply threats, and risk-off EM spillovers.

Sectors & Style

Energy, Commodities, and Inflation

  • OPEC+ decision and Iran's tanker seizure create substantial short-term risk for oil prices. Energy equities likely to remain volatile; input-cost pressure may impact transports and energy-dependent EMs.
  • Food tariff removals may temporarily ease US grocery input inflation; margin relief mainly for food importers/retail.

Consumer, Retail & Housing

  • US retail sales, confidence, and holiday data are coming—critical for travel/discretionary stocks and sector rotation near-term.
  • Persistent core inflation and higher-for-longer rates still overhang US housing, but any movement on cheaper, longer-dated mortgages or affordability reforms (50-year GSEs, discussed at the policy level) could spark a rotation; stay alert to headline-driven REIT volatility.
  • Travel/airlines set up for new Thanksgiving records; potential short-term bullishness for leisure/hospitality names.

Industrials/Autos

  • Tesla’s and GM’s US supply-chain policy (eliminating China-origin parts) may pressure Asian auto suppliers and benefit US/re-shoring manufacturers.
  • Ongoing defense budget news and Venezuela escalation support aerospace/defense spend expectations; watch for margin/contract delays if government shutdown materializes.

Tech, AI & Semiconductors

  • AI momentum intact, but leadership is increasingly competitive: Google Gemini 3 and other LLM advances (plus key earnings) could disrupt current narrative flows between NVDA/MSFT/GOOGL/Meta.
  • Hardware supply chain at risk if China-Japan or further US-China tensions escalate, especially for semiconductors.
  • Regulatory/antitrust and AI litigation (esp. OpenAI/Microsoft, ongoing DOJ/FTC posture) remain medium-term headline threats to mega-cap platforms.

Single-Name & Catalyst Watch

  • Nvidia (NVDA): AI/cloud spend and China risk are binaries at upcoming earnings; supply chain themes (TSMC, Arizona buildout) in focus.
  • Tesla (TSLA): EV demand, decoupling from Chinese supply, and FSD/robotaxi timing/penetration are all high-beta catalysts.
  • Apple (AAPL): Near-term focus on Gemini-OpenAI partnership rumor, guidance on services and supply chain shifts, risk from costlier new product launches (foldable, Vision Pro 2).
  • Meta/Alphabet/Amazon/MSFT: AI leadership, regulatory noise, and consumer signals from earnings drive directionality.
  • Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac: Talk of 50-year mortgages and potential IPO timelines can spur swings in REITs/mortgage finance.

Crypto & Alternatives

  • Short-term crypto flows are not broad-based positive; ETF inflows decelerating. Narrative leadership shifting among new L1/L2s, though sustained uptrend requires positive risk backdrop.
  • Stablecoin, DeFi, and altcoin airdrop schedules support isolated trading opportunities, but not broad market repricing.
  • Gold remains a credible long, especially as inflation and/or fiscal/sovereign risk rises—near-universal outperformance expected relative to BTC in 2025.

Event/Catalyst Watch

Major Scheduled Catalysts (Time-Sensitive)

  • Nov 2025 FOMC (5–6 Nov): Watch SEP/dot plot language for any move on 2026 cuts or long-term neutral rates.
  • Nov 2025 CPI & PPI (mid-Nov): Further downside surprise needed to justify risk-on; upside break (~3.1%) re-hawks rates.
  • US Government Funding Deadline: Shutdown brinkmanship or realization will shift sentiment defensively/increase T-bill/sovereign premium.
  • US Q4 Treasury Auctions: Any sign of failed auctions or bigger than expected sizes would bid up long yields—raising equity and REIT risk.
  • Key Tech/AI Earnings (Nov): Guide for cloud/AI demand, capex, and pricing power; surprises swing leadership between mega-caps and cyclicals.
  • OPEC+ Biannual Meeting (late Nov): Production decision determines oil path and input cost pressure for inflation, industrials, airlines, EMs.
  • Nov Jobs Report (first Dec week): Labor weakness shifts the Fed path decisively sooner.

Bottom Line

  • Macro conditions: Remain cross-currented. No support for easy policy; inflation sits above target and fiscal risk (debt/auction/shutdown) persists.
  • Positioning: Stick with quality, margin-resilient franchises. Remain neutral/underweight high-duration tech and levered cyclicals until rate/auction resolution is clear.
  • Risks: Global escalation events (oil, China, Venezuela); surprise policy/auction shock; government shutdown.
  • Opportunities: Tactical trades around inflation data, retail/holiday travel upside, defense/energy upside risk, selective FAANG/AI model leadership transitions.

Upcoming Catalysts (Near-Term Only)

  • Nov FOMC Meeting (5–6 Nov): Policy shift or inflation communications will set tone for rates and equities.
  • US CPI/PPI (mid-Nov): Further evidence of sticky inflation will pressure risk assets and adjust Fed path odds.
  • US Retail Sales/Confidence (late Nov): Weak consumer = support for defensives/duration; strength = good for fin/tech, delays cuts.
  • Q4 Treasury Auctions: Any sign of softness = higher yields, equity and REIT headwinds.
  • US Gov’t Shutdown Risk (late Nov): Event risk for US debt, contractors, and macro volatility; defensive bid possible.
