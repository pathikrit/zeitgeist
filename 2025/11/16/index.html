Macro & Market-Level

US Interest Rates/Yields

  • Fed and Global Cuts: The Fed is not expected to hike in 2025. First cut increasingly possible in late 2025 to early 2026, with odds steady for 2–3 cuts (possibly clustered) by end of 2026. ECB holds, BoE likely to cut in 2025.
    • TIPS/Treasuries: 10Y likely steady or rangebound for now; volatility clusters may surround CPI/inflation or funding headlines. Little evidence for a rate spike—soft landing regime remains base case.
    • Index/Equities: $SPY and $QQQ supported by strong megacap results and macro backdrop. Bearish fat-tail is possible but still out-of-the-money.

Inflation

  • CPI/PCE: October/Nov prints to confirm “sticky but stable” regime, 2.7–3.2%. Not a regime break, but keeps pricing power and sector rotation (commodities, midcap cyclicals) alive.
  • Wages: Wages seen outpacing inflation, supporting consumer spending through holiday.
  • Commodities: Gold to continue outperforming crypto as inflation hedge for 2025.

Fiscal/US Debt

  • Shutdown Risk: Short-term fiscal risk from government shutdown appears low but headline-driven volatility remains; focus on defense, healthcare, and government contractors if CR lapses.
  • Debt Ceiling: No new market-moving headlines, but US sovereign downgrade risk moderately elevated for 2025–2027.

Growth/Employment

  • GDP: Upside surprise in Q4 2025 growth (running 2–3.5%) likely; soft-landing still the dominant narrative.
  • Jobs: Payroll/ADP prints lagging as leading indicator, but labor market showing resilience; only a modest fade in hiring, not collapse.

Credit

  • Credit Markets: High-yield issuance and refinancing windows remain open; no funding freeze signals, but default risk is rising for small-cap HY/cyclicals if rates remain higher for long.

Thematic/Geopolitical

US Politics

  • Impeachment Risk: Ongoing impeachment talk (Trump) and legislative infighting could spike event volatility, but not a central scenario for equities or bonds absent further escalation.
  • Shutdowns, Fiscal Drift: No new shutdown until late Q4, but tax/budget gridlock and 2026 election balance-of-power remain overhangs.
  • ICE/Immigration: Immigration enforcement headlines add to risk aversion in hospitality, food, and regional REITs. Consumer staples (WMT/KR) could be pressured if broader policy narrative heats up.

Geopolitics/EM Risk

  • Middle East: Israel-Lebanon/Beirut strike odds remain above baseline; any large-scale Israeli action (or coordinated multi-front attack) would spike oil, defense, and gold—energy/commodities and defense/contractors are the only sectors with real tail-risk upside.
  • Russia/Ukraine: Russia offensive continues, no sign of short-term ceasefire—risk of escalation remains real, but base case is a grinding stalemate. Positive risk/reward for defense, ongoing headwind for European gas/oil.
  • China/Japan/Taiwan: China-Japan patrols and rhetoric up—not yet an acute FX or supply-chain shock, but odds for a near-term regional incident have moved higher. Keep tight risk management on semis and auto supply chains with Asia exposure.

Energy/Commodities

  • Oil: Range-bound unless Middle East or Russia-Ukraine narrative breaks. US and OPEC production coordination is keeping prices capped, with potential for a headline shock into the next OPEC+ meeting (early December).
  • Gold: Outperforming crypto on safe-haven/inflation hedge sequencing—no reversal visible in the near term given policy regime and soft landing macro.
  • Agriculture: Focus on Latin American risk (Mexico/Peru protests, Amazon mining, etc.) for select food/commodity plays.

Key Takeaways for Positioning

  • Remain constructive on US equities with cyclical tilt, overweight megacap tech/AI and select financials, but hedge for event-driven tail risk from geopolitical spikes and US fiscal/political headlines.
  • Own gold and TIPS for residual inflation risk—still superior to crypto near-term.
  • Keep energy, defense exposure as underpriced insurance vs. sharp exogenous shocks (OPEC/MENA escalation).
  • Homebuilders/residential REITs have favorable tailwinds into next macro inflections; trim exposure to heavily urban/office REITs facing event-driven social/political drag.
  • Crypto: Stay tactical, not levered; short-term momentum traders may fade, regulatory event catalysts likely delayed.
  • Monitor upcoming catalysts closely for regime breaks—most macro risk is concentrated in exogenous/geopolitical event clusters or an unlikely sharp inflation break. Broad paradigm is "soft landing until proven otherwise," but headline risk is elevated and event-driven volatility is priced cheaply.
