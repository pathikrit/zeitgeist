Macroeconomic & Bond Market Outlook

Fiscal & Monetary Policy

  • U.S. government spending continues to rise meaningfully, with a strong likelihood of another year with an increase of at least $500B and a notable chance of $1T+. The probability of a U.S. budget shutdown remains significant (roughly 40%), signaling risk to Q4 government payments and GDP.
  • Treasury issuance and U.S. national debt trajectories project debt above $38T by year’s end, keeping long-term bond yields pressured and sovereign-risk narratives active. Short-term risk of U.S. credit rating downgrade is present, albeit not base-case.
  • The Fed is seen as highly likely to cut rates at least once this year, with around two cuts being the modal outcome. Market doesn't expect emergency moves or hikes; Jackson Hole and inflation data remain key catalysts for fixed income volatility.

Inflation, Employment & Real Economy

  • Headline and core CPI are projected to stay in the 2.6–3.5% range for 2025, with elevated but not runaway inflation. This supports modest nominal growth, but suggests the Fed will keep real rates near neutral.
  • Unemployment is expected to remain just above 4%, indicating a soft but not recessionary labor market. Tech sector layoffs could tick up from 2024, suggesting continued divergence in labor market strength across sectors.
  • Recession probability remains modest for 2025 but is not ruled out later into 2025—watch for Q3 as a potential inflection.

Commodities & Energy

  • Oil price distribution implies risk of significant downside: scenarios with WTI in the $65–$50 range are well represented. This reflects either an economic slowdown, supply increases, or both. Sharp spikes above $85 are not base-case, dampening the inflation impulse from energy.

Sector View

Real Estate

  • Major U.S. housing markets (NYC, SF) continue to show rising rent trajectories; NYC expected to outpace SF, possibly by a wide margin. National real estate returns will be robust in urban multifamily and rent-linked REITs, with inflation-protected lease structures outperforming.
    • Weather risks increasing (hurricane season, floods), with implications for property insurers and local municipal bonds.

Technology

  • AI legal and privacy risk highlighted by class actions (Otter AI). Additionally, OpenAI faces a strong risk of high-value tort claims in the coming years; risk premium rising for high-valuation private/soon-to-be-public AI names.
  • Multiple indicators point to likely increases in layoffs for big tech in 2025, potentially highlighting maturation or overinvestment in prior years.
  • IPO watch: The market expects OpenAI to go public before Anthropic, but litigation, regulatory, and governance events could affect timing and structure.
  • Hardware innovation (tiny SSDs, chip cycles) signals competition for U.S./China supply chains, but no immediate systemic fallout.

Healthcare

  • Hospital/insurer complex benefits from strong capital flow (Berkshire Hathaway stake in UnitedHealth). Managed care and insurance may outperform as demographic and cost inflation trends favor scale.
  • Increased focus on mental health crisis (children in ERs, rising need for services); policy and payor side could shift, leading to new investment in behavioral health and outpatient infrastructure.
  • Outbreaks (NYC Legionnaires’ disease) and pandemic tail risk remain, but healthcare adaptation (home vaccine delivery, new protocols) reduces systemic risk.

Crypto & Alternatives

  • Crypto assets, especially Bitcoin and Ethereum, continue to show speculative upside, but with high realized volatility. The likelihood of another Tether/USDT crisis is very low for 2025, but legal and regulatory tail risks persist.
  • ETF approvals (Dogecoin, Ripple) keep speculative retail engagement alive, supporting volumes. Watch for rotation in token leadership and possible new product launches as signals of sentiment.
  • Gold and alternative hedges remain relevant as fiscal/sovereign concerns and mild inflation persist.

Defense/Geopolitics

  • Russia/Ukraine peace looks unlikely in the short run. Risk premium persists for defense, energy, and agri-related sectors, although immediate escalation isn't base-case.
  • U.S.-China/Taiwan standoff risk rhetorically de-escalated but remains a medium-term concern.
  • Ongoing turbulence in Middle East (Israel/Gaza/Sudan) and state-recognition politics could increase volatility in EM assets and commodities, but not immediate contagion risk for U.S.-centric equities.

Individual Names & Events

  • UnitedHealth: Boosted by Buffett’s Berkshire stake—watch for spillovers across managed care, insurance, and healthcare indices.
  • Applied Materials, Sandisk, Pixel 10/Google: Hardware/product cycle theme persists. Cycles matter in tech; watch for capital flow and earnings momentum.
  • Otter.AI and other AI software companies: Elevated headline legal risk—PR and compliance now critical for enterprise adoption and partnerships.
  • OpenAI: Monetization and liability risks are increasing; IPO timing likely coming before Anthropic, but headline/regulatory shocks remain possible.
  • Major eventual IPOs (OpenAI/Anthropic): The race for first AI IPO is heating up; whichever entity comes to market first may set the tone on valuation, sector multiples, and governance standards.
  • REITs/Urban Real Estate: Renter demand and inflation linkages favor NYC/SF landlords and urban-focused funds in the near-term, especially as wage-supportive inflation persists.
  • Energy/Insurers: Active hurricane season and commodity volatility favor nimble hedgers and underwriters with strong weather risk models.

Forward Watchlist

  • Fed Chair Powell’s Jackson Hole commentary for direction on rates; summer CPI data for confirmation of inflation path.
  • Track urban rent trend data, especially NYC vs SF, for clues to consumer stress/urban migration/REIT health.
  • Ongoing tech layoffs, especially in prominent players, for signals on sector rotation.
  • Political redistricting and D.C./federal/local confrontations for macro-political risk.
  • Geopolitical developments in Eastern Europe and Middle East for inflation/energy/EM shock transmission.

In summary: U.S. equities are underpinned by strong earnings in select sectors (healthcare, technology hardware, urban real estate) and a generally supportive macro regime (no recession, modest Fed easing). However, risks remain elevated in federal fiscal management, international geopolitics, and sector-specific disruptions (AI, tech legal/labor). Watch for increased volatility driven by weather events, labor conflicts, political intervention, and upcoming macro data releases.
