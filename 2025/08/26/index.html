Macro Outlook

US Economy & Fed Policy

  • The US growth outlook appears moderate, with GDP most likely to grow around 1–2.5% in 2025, and core inflation expected in the 3–3.5% range.
  • Market consensus is for the Federal Reserve to begin lowering rates by the end of 2025, with multiple cuts likely by December, though deep or aggressive easing remains improbable.
  • Unemployment could edge higher this fall, creating possible headwinds for consumer sectors.
  • The US national debt is set to top $38 trillion by year-end, with high confidence in further annual increases, raising concerns over long-term fiscal sustainability and bond market supply.

Actionable themes:

  • Continue to watch for upward pressure on longer-term yields; duration and inflation hedges remain prudent.
  • Credit spreads and risk premiums could widen if growth disappoints, or if political drama around the Fed escalates.

Global Risks & Currency Markets

  • France’s political turmoil threatens to destabilize EU financial markets and the euro, prompting risk-off shifts into USD and treasuries in the near term.
  • Moderate ongoing de-dollarization pressure, but USD Index remains resilient barring a severe global crisis.
  • Significant geopolitical flashpoints in Europe (France, Ukraine conflict), Asia-Pacific (China, Australia-Iran tensions), and the Middle East generate higher uncertainty premiums for global assets, commodities, and defense-related sectors.

Actionable themes:

  • EUR/USD likely to remain soft; monitor for breakout risk if French instability deepens.
  • Exposure to global commodities (especially energy), defense, and select EM risk premia adjustments warranted.

Sectors & Thematic

Technology

  • Tech remains under a regulatory and antitrust cloud, both in the US and EU.
    • High likelihood courts will declare both Amazon and Apple as monopolies before 2030, with Live Nation facing similar challenges.
    • FTC scrutiny could intensify for Tesla, Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta, and Amazon, with possible reputational and operational overhangs—but with slow, drawn-out legal timelines.
  • Nvidia is favored to be the world’s top market cap by year-end, reaffirming leadership in AI/semis; Microsoft and Alphabet will continue to lag in relative size.
  • Apple’s impending iPhone 17 launch could drive short-term volatility; cyclicality following product launches is a risk.
  • IPO pipeline is sluggish among high-profile tech names (OpenAI, Stripe, Starlink), with nearer-term anticipation for Klarna and OpenAI; Anduril (defense) may debut in 2026.

Actionable themes:

  • Continue overweight to leading AI/semis; prepare for rotation on major tech event/news.
  • Remain cautious on Big Tech antitrust risks.
  • Monitor for defense, crypto, and select fintech IPO activity in 2026.

Energy & Commodities

  • Oil and coal expected to remain substantial contributors to global primary energy through 2030; transition to renewables will be incremental and region-dependent.
  • Ukraine’s attacks on Russian refineries may create asymmetric upside shocks in energy and fertilizer prices.
  • The US is unlikely to meet 2030 climate targets; India has a higher probability of progress relative to the US and EU, implying policy tailwinds for Indian renewables and infrastructure plays.

Actionable themes:

  • Tactical overweight to energy/commodities, especially on further supply shocks.
  • Indian renewables and infrastructure appear well-positioned for multiyear policy and capital flows.

Financials

  • JPMorgan CEO transition remains open, with internal succession likely.
  • Market is alert to further Fed personnel shakeups, which may trigger volatility in rates and the financial sector.
  • Deutsche Bank-style idiosyncratic Euro bank risk could rise if EU political fragmentation deepens.

Actionable themes:

  • Preference for US/global systemically important banks; monitor for adverse news in Eurozone banking sector.

Crypto & Alternative Assets

  • Outlook for leading crypto assets is moderately bullish, with a good probability of further upside in Bitcoin and Ethereum by year-end, but extreme upside appears overstretched.
  • Crypto ETFs (Ripple, Dogecoin) likely to expand available retail/insto access in 2025; expect inflows, secondary volatility.
  • Crypto infra (DeFi, perps) expansion is progressing; regulatory environment remains key.

Actionable themes:

  • Maintain core crypto/ETH allocation; consider selective increases on favorable ETF approval news.
  • DeFi exposure should be adaptive to regulatory changes and new product launches.

Company-Specific

  • Nvidia is strongly positioned as the likely top-cap global company; continued AI momentum remains a sector-wide driver.
  • Apple faces minimal CEO risk, but price action post-iPhone 17 announcement should be monitored for mean reversions.
  • Amazon and Apple face significant antitrust legal battles; long-term valuation impact likely muted but watch for episodic volatility.
  • OpenAI: Expected to IPO before Anthropic; NYT lawsuit combined with potential >$1M tort payout could shift regulatory debate and impact legal/lobbying costs across the AI sector.
  • AT&T’s $23B spectrum deal signals commitment to scale and compete in 5G—potentially negative for EchoStar’s rivals and synergistic for telcos and hardware partners.

Other Risks & Notables

  • Natural disasters (earthquake in Japan, hurricanes Karen/Sebastien) pose moderate insurance and real-asset downside in affected geographies, though US/CA earthquake tail risks remain remote in the near term.
  • No imminent risk of Schengen or EU disintegration, but political instability (France, Hungary, Italy) raises volatility in EU assets.
  • Unlikely that China will overtake the US economy by 2030, but emerging markets remain sensitive to US-China/EU rate, FX, and trade headlines.

Summary Positioning

  • Stay overweight leading AI/semiconductors, Indian renewables, and US energy/commodities.
  • Maintain flexible rates and duration exposure, with a tilt toward risk-management as political/fiscal volatility rises into US and EU election cycles.
  • Monitor regulatory scrutiny in Big Tech and fintech; stay tactical around major IPO, product, or legal events.
  • Stay alert to geopolitical and commodity disruptions, balancing risk-adjusted return across global opportunity sets.
