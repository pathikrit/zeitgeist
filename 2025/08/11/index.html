Macro Overview

US Monetary Policy & Rates

  • Fed is expected to begin cutting rates this year, with at least 2 rate cuts looking likely by year-end. There remains very low risk of an emergency rate cut or hike, and extremely low chance for no movement.
  • By mid-2026, the policy rate is unlikely to be much above 3.25%. By end-2026, higher rates are extremely unlikely—reinforcing a lower-for-longer regime into the next cycle.
  • Inflation is expected to remain somewhat sticky in the 2.5–3.5% range for 2025, with core inflation trending slightly above CPI. A resurgence to >4% or collapse below 2% appears unlikely this year.
  • US GDP growth is anticipated to moderate, majority view clustering in 1.1–2.5% for 2025 (below trend, but not recessionary). Odds of recession this year are low, though not negligible. Unemployment set to rise further, likely above 4%.

Implications:

  • Strategically overweight duration and rate-sensitive assets as aggressive tightening risks are now remote and normalization is underway.
  • Inflation-protected strategies remain relevant, but upside inflation “shock” plays are now less urgent.
  • Macro remains moderately constructive for risk, but rule out “roaring 2021-style” growth.

US Debt/Fiscal Position

  • US debt to continue rising, with the national balance highly likely to exceed $38T by year-end, reinforcing secular deficit trends. No immediate risk of a US credit event, but probability of another rating downgrade has ticked somewhat higher.
  • Government shutdowns and brinksmanship risk remains; potential for intermittent volatility and sectoral impact (defense, contractors, public services).

Sector Perspective

Technology & AI

  • Tech earnings face caution as labor markets remain tight—expect tech layoffs in 2025 to outpace 2024.
  • US semiconductor production growth is strong (visible >7% YoY), aided by policy, but with geopolitical overhang: risk of US-China chip detente re-emerging.
  • New US policy (Trump stance) signals willingness to “tax” AI chip sales to China, rather than outright ban—this could alter revenue/profit mix for Nvidia, AMD, and other AI-exposed firms, potentially supportive in the near term but uncertain over strategic horizon.
  • AI benchmarks & regulatory risks remain a background threat; headline risks for OpenAI, Google (Alphabet), Amazon leadership changes are low.
  • Apple/iPhone 17 launch (higher price, thinner) and Google hardware expansions (Pixel Pen) continue to drive incremental hardware cycles, but margin pressure persists.

Implications:

  • Near-term tailwind for select US chipmakers; positive for AI-infrastructure ecosystem if export strategies open to “regulated” China access.
  • Monitor risk of margin compression and cyclical tech employment resets.

Energy, Green, & Industrials

  • Strong velocity of US solar installations in 2025 (exceeding 30 GW expected, with high confidence), but targets well above 50 GW look aspirational.
  • Ford’s aggressive EV pivot (cheaper trucks, streamlined production) aligns the sector for competitive price reductions—potential for margin compression.
  • Steel supply chains (US Steel explosion) under temporary stress; possible near-term price support in steel commodities, ripple risk for manufacturing delays/downstream users.
  • Little probability for new US nuclear reactor licenses, so no near-term catalyst for nuclear-heavy utilities.

Implications:

  • Green sector plays—especially solar—will do well, but “hyper-growth” scenarios such as massive 70 GW+ annual installations unlikely.
  • Industrial/materials equities could see episodic volatility from supply chain events/disasters.

Healthcare & Cannabis

  • Trump administration expected to clarify federal cannabis rescheduling policy soon, with a significant (though not certain) chance of rescheduling. This remains a material catalyst for the sector, supportive of cannabis equities and potentially hospitals (if descheduling eases banking/financing/friction).
  • New “off-the-shelf” cancer vaccine breakthroughs spotlight innovation premium in biotech, but no imminent “market-moving” approval.
  • mRNA vaccine funding controversies could impact pharma short-term, but second- and third-order effects (innovation slowdown, political risk premium) are for the longer-term watch list.

Financials & Real Assets

  • Gold: Overwhelming odds of gold ending 2025 well above $2,500/oz; strong possibility of gold reaching/exceeding $3,200 as de-dollarization, deficit, and risk-hedge flows persist.
