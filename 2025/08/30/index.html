Daily Memo (30-Aug-2025)

News Highlights

  • Trump Tariffs: Appeals court rules Trump's tariffs illegal but allows them to stay in place for now, creating policy uncertainty.
  • Secret Service: Trump administration ends security detail for former VP Kamala Harris, raising questions about norms and security protocols.
  • CDC Leadership: New CDC head appointed with controversial, non-medical background as previous chief ousted amidst political struggle.
  • Deportation Policy: Federal judge blocks Trump’s attempt to expand fast-track deportations, potentially slowing mass removal efforts.
  • Foreign Aid & Spending Fights: Trump cancels $4.9B in foreign aid, escalating budget tensions with Congress and risking government shutdown.
  • End of ‘De Minimis’ Tariff Loophole: Small-value imports now subject to standard US tariffs, likely to disrupt global retail and e-commerce.
  • Fed Board Turmoil: Ongoing legal fight over Trump’s attempt to remove a Federal Reserve governor, suggesting possible instability at the Fed.
  • Momentum in US Equities: US markets showing strong momentum into September.
  • Major Product Launches: Apple and Google both set for new device launches, fueling tech sector focus.

Macro & Policy

US Fed & Rates

  • The consensus continues to indicate high likelihood of Fed rate cuts later this year or early next, with almost no expectation of rate hikes or an emergency cut. While two cuts are possible, expectations are modest.

    • Bond market: This dynamic supports a gradual move toward lower yields, slightly benefiting longer-duration bonds, but without sudden dislocations.
    • Equities: Lower rates should serve as a near-term tailwind, especially for growth sectors and higher-multiple stocks.
    • Ongoing legal and political battles over Fed board appointments could inject episodic volatility if Trump succeeds in reshaping the board’s composition or policies.

  • Recession Risk: Market still sees a US recession this year as unlikely. Corporate guidance and consumer spending trends will need monitoring, but the base case remains economic resilience.

  • Trump Administration Policy:

    • Fiscal tightening is unlikely in the near term, as is meaningful progress on corporate or high-earner tax cuts.
    • Disputes over federal spending and foreign aid signal ongoing policy uncertainty, especially with added risk of shutdowns or spending disruptions.
    • Judicial rulings may weaken Trump’s leverage over tariffs and migration, slowing or derailing major policy shifts.

  • Trade: Legal challenges to tariffs inject uncertainty, especially combined with the end of de minimis exemptions for cheap imports. This will likely:

    • Increase the cost of imported goods, fueling inflation for low-margin retailers and e-commerce.
    • Complicate global supply chains and create friction specifically for US-based consumer brands and logistics providers.

Geopolitics & International

  • Russia/Ukraine: Ceasefire prospects for 2025 remain unlikely, so commodity and defense sector impacts continue, supporting high energy prices and persistent defense spending.

  • China, Modi, Putin Meeting: The strengthening front against the US may increase trade and geopolitical tensions, affecting global capital flows and multinational earnings.

  • Middle East & Iran: Extremely low probability of breakthrough with Iran or major shifts in energy geopolitics, but always worth watching for tail risk events.

  • Emerging Markets:

    • Brazil’s job market looks relatively strong, supporting assets in the region compared to riskier EM peers.
    • No immediate triggers out of Africa or the Middle East for market-wide moves.

Climate/Natural Disaster

  • Above-normal Atlantic hurricane activity is expected this season, elevating risk for insurers, reinsurers, and East Coast real estate, but barring a major landfall event, the impact is containment.

Sectors

Technology

  • The tech sector remains buoyant thanks to anticipated Fed cuts and robust product cycles:

    • Apple is front and center with imminent iPhone 17 launches, likely to provide a positive catalyst for both the company and its supply chain.
    • Google garners positive early reviews for the Pixel 10, supporting themes in AI-driven hardware.
    • General AI and device refresh cycle optimism continues to drive momentum.

  • Legal and regulatory risks persist around leadership changes in tech-relevant government agencies (e.g., CDC, DOJ), but no near-term, sector-wide regulatory shock is likely.

Consumer & Retail

  • The removal of the 'de minimis' import exemption could cause price increases and margin pressures for US retailers relying on direct-from-China/Asia shipments.

    • Watch for weaker guidance, especially from large e-commerce and discount clothing/consumer goods retailers.
    • Potential share gain for US-based, vertically integrated, or less import-dependent brands.

  • Entertainment and streaming remain stable, driven by consumer resilience indicators, but no single event likely to shift the sector near term.

Healthcare

  • CDC turmoil could impact COVID vaccine distribution and regulatory clarity, but sector fundamentals are unchanged unless new health crises emerge.

Energy & Commodities

  • No imminent licensing for new US nuclear reactors; energy markets remain supported by ongoing geopolitical risk and no near-term supply shocks.
  • Oil prices likely to remain firm given geopolitical events and continued Russian conflict.

Financials

  • No major risk of disorderly events for leveraged crypto plays (such as MicroStrategy) or US banks from current policy, but volatility remains in play if policy or legal headlines intensify.

Stocks & Individual Names

  • Apple (AAPL): Likely to outperform short term on iPhone 17 event and continued consumer interest, barring supply chain surprises from new tariffs.
  • Google (GOOGL/GOOG): Positive momentum from device reviews and AI positioning.
  • MicroStrategy (MSTR): Limited immediate risk of margin call; underlying Bitcoin price remains the more volatile driver.
  • Tesla (TSLA), Magnificent 7: No imminent threat of shrinking their index weight; status quo supports continued leadership by mega caps.

Trades & Tactical Themes

  • Bias toward risk assets in the near term with dovish Fed and strong equity momentum outweighing macro/policy risk.
  • Cautious on pure retail importers and global e-commerce reliant on low-tariff imports; shift toward companies with domestic supply chains could benefit from new tariffs.
  • Defense and energy sectors continue to benefit from a prolonged Russia/Ukraine conflict and general geopolitical risk premium.
  • Longer-duration bonds may offer tactical value with likely Fed easing, but spreads and inflation expectations must be monitored carefully.

Key Risks To Monitor:

  • Sudden policy moves on tariffs, budget shutdown risk, or trade retaliation.
  • Outlier events: natural disaster, new pandemic wave, legal escalation against Fed or other institutions.
  • Volatility from government, monetary policy, and leadership shakeups in key agencies.
