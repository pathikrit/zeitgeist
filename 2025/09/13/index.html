Macro & Monetary Policy

Federal Reserve Policy Outlook

  • The market overwhelmingly expects multiple Fed rate cuts in 2025; the most likely scenario is two or three cuts, with little chance of rate hikes or an emergency cut.
  • There is broad confidence that the US will not enter a recession in 2025, and the probability of a marketwide circuit breaker event is seen as low.

Implications:

  • Lower and/or falling rates should support US broad equity valuations, especially cash-generative and tech names, and lower overall market volatility unless unexpected shocks occur.
  • Expect further downward pressure on yields, particularly at the front and middle of the Treasury curve, with duration risk largely contained barring new shocks.
  • TIPS and inflation hedges remain a low priority; risk of inflation above 10% is dismissed by markets.

Fiscal Policy & US Default Risk

  • Very low expectation of US budget cuts or debt default. Markets are not pricing in fiscal discipline or near-term sovereign stress.
  • Macro and rates policy are unlikely to face big volatility from debt ceiling or shutdown brinkmanship in 2025.

Legislative & Political Uncertainty

  • Republican Party is viewed as highly likely to win Senate control in 2026; House much less certain, implying potential for legislative gridlock but a rightward shift.
  • Trump's administration faces low probability for high-impact removal or resignation outcomes this year; Fed Chair succession remains uncertain, with a number of mainstream candidates but no clear favorite yet.

2nd order: Expect policy outcomes to be more stable than headlines suggest, but longer-term changes to energy, healthcare, and tech policy should be re-evaluated post-election.
