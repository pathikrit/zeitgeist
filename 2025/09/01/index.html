Macro & Policy

US Inflation, Rates & Fiscal Position

  • Stubborn Inflation: Most likely, US inflation will remain above 3% for 2025, with core measures also persistent. This indicates ongoing price pressures and challenges for households and businesses, especially in rate-sensitive sectors (real estate, consumer durables).
  • Fed Policy Outlook: Multiple cuts are possible in 2025, but more than two is unlikely; a "wait and see" stance dominates. Rate hike or emergency cut scenarios are very low probability.
  • US 10Y Treasury Yield: While spikes >4.6% are not base case, upward pressure remains plausible due to sticky inflation and rising debt. Yields north of 4% remain a risk for duration-heavy bond exposures.
  • Fiscal Dynamics: US debt is likely to surpass $38 trillion by end-2025. No sign of resolution—attention warranted to potential crowding out, effects on the dollar, and government financing costs.

Implications:

  • Real rates may rise slower than hoped; TIPS and short-duration bonds still merit consideration.
  • Persistent inflation supports cost-push pricing power in equities, but margin pressure will rise for labor-intensive and heavily leveraged firms.
  • Fiscal strain: tail risk for currency depreciation and potential shift in appetite for US sovereign debt.

Global Growth & Recession Chances

  • US Recession in 2025/6: Unlikely in 2025, but odds climb moderately thereafter, especially with softness in key trading partners (Europe, Japan, UK).
  • GDP Growth: US growth to continue but slow, with base case for 1–2.5% in 2025. A soft landing remains probable.
  • Labor Market: Unemployment creeping higher, reflecting less robust jobs growth, but catastrophic losses unlikely; wage growth may barely outpace inflation.
  • China/EM: China’s manufacturing weak but stabilizing; India/China relationship warming could support regional trade, but global demand remains soft.

Implications:

  • Defensive US exposure, selective overweighting of services/healthcare/utilities.
  • EM currencies (peso, real) face continued depreciation; commodity exposure offers only tactical relief.
  • US-listed MNCs with China/India exposure face more regulatory/political risk than immediate trade upside.

Climate & Energy Policy

  • Climate Goals Slippage: US and EU both unlikely to hit announced 2030 targets; India is a relative bright spot. Global 2°C threshold will likely be breached well before 2050.
  • Energy Mix: Oil and coal remain dominant in 2030's energy mix, despite renewable investment. Fossil fuel transition will underwhelm popular expectations.
  • Inflation Effects: Persistent climate failings and energy shocks may further pressure prices, especially in insurance, food, and utilities.

Sectors and Themes

Technology & AI

  • AI IPO Watch: OpenAI is the leading candidate for a large tech IPO, but no announcement is imminent. Watch for capital markets risk-on/risk-off sentiment swings, especially as high-profile IPO windows can drive cyclical sector rotations.
  • Tech Layoffs: Substantial increase in layoffs anticipated this year—high valuations and over-extended payrolls are being trimmed. This could further pressure consumer discretionary and urban real estate.
  • Enterprise AI Race: Google and xAI look dominant in near-term large-model leadership, with Meta and Anthropic as challengers. AI hardware up-cycle continues: US chip production growth >7% is highly probable.
  • Antitrust: Both Amazon and Apple likely to face adverse judicial rulings related to monopoly status; continued regulatory headwinds for large consumer platforms.

Implications:

  • Favor upstream tech (semis, specialized compute, data infrastructure) over end-user consumer tech.
  • Potential derating for mega-cap platforms; rotational flows towards growth-at-a-reasonable-price and non-advertising-reliant software names.
  • Layoffs provide further leverage for margin expansion, but signal weaker broader demand/advertising.

Energy, Resources & Climate

  • Commodities: Oil prices face downside pressure year-end (low 60s/bbl possible), though whiplash volatility cannot be ruled out. Natural gas likely to be higher, reflecting supply constraints or extreme weather.
  • Renewables: Solar installation momentum remains strong in the US, but unlikely to hit most optimistic targets for capacity expansion.
  • Energy Storage (Tesla): Battery/energy storage growth solid, but oversized near-term jumps are improbable.
  • Utilities: Relative winners with persistent power demand, but capex and regulatory risk rising due to slow transition away from fossil fuels.

Implications:

  • Short/mid-term energy rotation can be tactical, especially in natural gas and grid infrastructure.
  • Watch for underperformance from pure-play green tech bets (relative to expectations) until acceleration in policy or physical climate shocks.

Real Estate

  • Housing/Rents: Rents continue to climb in major cities (NYC, SF), though not at runaway rates. Higher rates and sticky inflation keep housing affordability tight—rental REITs favored over homebuilders.
  • Commercial Real Estate: Implications from tech layoffs—less robust demand for urban office space.

Individual Names & Events

Company Specific

  • Apple: Product cycle focus remains on iPhones and OS updates; hardware innovation (e.g., cellular laptops) likely, but major new category expansion is improbable this year. Regulatory pressure (monopoly findings) is an increasing headwind.
  • Tesla: Truck production will grow, but no breakthrough on very low-cost models; delivery and energy storage numbers likely to disappoint versus early bull hopes. Remain selective, favoring supply chain or megatrend partners over direct auto play.
  • Amazon & Live Nation: Both under antitrust scrutiny; risk of regulatory-induced business model changes or fines is high.
  • Netflix: Price hikes possible but not certain; content spend and margin management to be closely watched.
  • Crypto (Bitcoin, Ripple, USDC, Tether): High volatility; upside to $130k BTC is plausible in 2025 but higher targets (>150k) look stretched. Institutional acceptance (Ripple ETF) likely. Some depegging risk for major stablecoins, but not near systemic levels.

Geopolitics & Other

  • Middle East/Eastern Europe: Events (Gaza, Ukraine, Yemen) add to global tail risks—materials/energy/defense sectors must hedge for event-driven volatility.
  • China/India: Recent partnership announcements reduce immediate escalation risk, but do not remove longer-term manufacturing/trade reshuffling.

Key Watch Points

  • Inflation and Fed cuts path: Two rate cuts most likely, but stickiness in core CPI can delay or limit magnitude—stagger rate/duration exposure accordingly.
  • Tech layoffs cascade: Indirect effects on advertising, spending, and urban service demand.
  • Antitrust and regulatory risk: Platform valuation resets possible—embrace dispersion opportunities.
  • Energy transition patience: Position for lumpy, policy-dependent adoption curves in green tech.
  • Currency and EM devaluation: Emerging market FX under pressure; allocate accordingly.
  • Geopolitical surprises: Maintain some optionality for rapidly escalating conflict or supply shocks.

Summary:
Prepare for a market environment defined by sticky inflation, gradual disinflationary policy, continued pressure on mega-cap tech valuations, modest earnings growth, geopolitical flare-ups, and bumpy progress on decarbonization. Tilt towards defensiveness, selective growth (AI infrastructure), tactical commodity/utility exposure, and barbell duration positioning. Avoid chasing narrative-driven upside in high-profile names without clear catalysts.
