Macro & Asset Allocation

US Economy & Markets

  • Recession Odds: The macro outlook points to a high likelihood of a soft landing, with low odds of a US recession or emergency rate cuts in 2025.
  • Growth: GDP growth is most likely to moderate in the 1.1–2.5% band, with near-term projections for Q3 confirming mild but positive expansion.
  • Inflation: Consumer price inflation and core inflation are expected to remain above target, settling mainly in the 2.6–3.5% range, suggesting real yields could stay slightly positive and pressuring bond prices.
  • Unemployment: Moderate uptick in unemployment (above 4.1%) is projected by late 2025, reflecting a softening but not collapsing job market.
  • Fed Policy: Multiple rate cuts are anticipated for 2025, with at least two cuts a significant base case. However, very aggressive (four or more) cuts look unlikely, which tempers expectations for a drastic bond rally.
  • US Debt: National debt continues to grow, with $38 trillion+ by year-end almost certain. Fiscal pressure, including potential government shutdowns, could weigh on USD and long-duration bonds, while risk premia may inch up for Treasuries in 2H25 and beyond.
  • USD Outlook: De-dollarization risk is debated, but consensus leans toward a weaker dollar in 2025. Emerging market currencies, especially MXN and BRL, appear vulnerable, while EUR/USD could see large swings but no existential Eurozone risk in the short run.

International/EM

  • Geopolitics: Russia–Ukraine tensions and potential for Western troop escalation drive risk aversion across European assets. Africa’s new Ebola outbreak could stifle nascent investor optimism in select frontier markets.
  • Europe: EU disintegration fears remain low to moderate (France, Italy, Hungary sporadically in focus), but FX and rate volatility possible around elections/referenda.

Sectors & Thematic Exposures

Technology & AI

  • Cloud/AI: Anthropic’s $1.5B legal settlement is manageable for big tech but supports ongoing IP cost inflation in AI. Apple’s hardware event and sector leadership remain secure, particularly as cellular-enabled Macbooks draw closer.
  • Semiconductors: US semiconductor production is expected to post strong double-digit growth in 2025 amid robust AI demand, supporting related equities and capex-heavy names.
  • Mega-Cap Tech: The rotation within the ‘Magnificent 7’ appears sticky; Nvidia stands poised to become the world’s largest company by year-end, reinforcing semiconductor and cloud hardware themes.
  • Index Dynamics: High probability of names like AppLovin and Robinhood joining the S&P 500, suggesting trading tailwinds for these stocks and some realignment among “indexers.”
  • Succession/Corporate Risk: Leadership transitions at Alphabet, Apple, Amazon are low-probability risks for 2025.

Healthcare/Biotech

  • Drug Pricing/Reform: Continued downward pressure on pricing for popular classes (Ozempic, insulin). Expect modest price reductions, challenging profitability for specialty pharma; this pressure may accelerate with US election rhetoric.
  • FDA Pipeline: Drug approval activity robust but unlikely to produce game-changing “cures” in the near term. Speculative biotechs may continue to underperform large-cap, platform-based pharma.
  • Vaccine Policy: Attempts at vaccine rollbacks lack momentum, but pandemic risk remains in view with news of new Ebola outbreaks.

Energy, Commodities & Renewables

  • Oil & Gas: WTI oil likely faces downside risks into late 2025, with renewed pressure from global slowdown and resilient supply. However, volatility is heightened and meaningful energy rallies can’t be ruled out on geopolitics.
  • Natural Gas: Bullish bias for 2025 with prices solidly above $3–4.
  • Renewables: US solar installation is likely to clear 30 GW but faces resistance at higher targets. Renewables should keep outpacing utilities/energy peers despite cost/inflation headwinds.

Real Estate & REITs

  • Residential Trends: Rent inflation in both San Francisco and New York City looks set to continue, especially in NYC. Rising rents support multi-family and urban REIT names but could also draw rebuke from policy-makers.
  • Catastrophe Risk: Elevated earthquake risk is being debated (California, Japan); while tail risks are remote, insurance and catastrophe-exposed REITs may experience episodic volatility.
  • Urban Recovery: NYC transit usage returning to near pre-pandemic levels supports commercial and transportation-linked real estate.

Individual Names & Special Situations

  • Tesla (TSLA): Shareholder approval for Musk’s major new pay package looks highly unlikely. Ongoing governance and key-person risk persists; media scrutiny around pay could weigh on sentiment.
  • Nvidia (NVDA): Strong prospects for maintaining or reaching number one global market cap by year-end, underpinning demand in AI/semis.
  • Apple (AAPL): New hardware cycle, especially with iPhone 17 and possible higher device prices. CEO risk appears minimal.
  • Robinhood (HOOD) & AppLovin (APP): High index inclusion probabilities for S&P 500 suggest potential for outsized passive flows and retail investor attention.
  • SpaceX: Sustained high launch cadence continues to support the broader US aerospace and industrial complex.

Crypto & Alternatives

  • Bitcoin: Moderately positive outlook, with substantial probability of breaching $130,000 by end-2025 but limited odds for much higher levels. Drawdowns below $70,000 and flash crashes remain non-trivial but not base case.
  • Ethereum: Path to $5,000 remains plausible, but $10,000 this year is unlikely. Multiple new crypto ETFs (Ripple, Doge, Cardano, Litecoin) set to launch, facilitating mainstream access but potentially muting returns due to dilution and retail overhang.
  • Solana and other alt-L1s: No meaningful flippening events expected in short run.
  • Gold/TIPS: Steady core inflation supports persistent, if not spectacular, hedging flows.

Tactical Risks & Watch Items

  • US Government Shutdown: Elevated risk remains; investors should anticipate volatility in Treasuries, potential credit spreads widening.
  • Catastrophic Events: Major quakes in California/Japan or extreme Covid/pandemic resurgence have low but non-negligible probabilities; remain alert for outlier scenarios.
  • Geopolitical Shock: Ukraine escalation, US–China standoff, or Eurozone crisis flare-up could provide macro volatility spikes not priced into rates markets.

Takeaway:
The general investment climate remains favorable for risk assets, led by US tech and semiconductors, with continued moderate growth and inflation. However, vigilance is warranted as macro, policy, and idiosyncratic risks (politics, rate cycle, catastrophic events) could change the outlook quickly. Focus on sector rotation, index inclusions, and key legislative/catastrophe triggers. Stay nimble to evolving bond, currency, and geopolitical themes.
