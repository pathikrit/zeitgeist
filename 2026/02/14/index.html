Daily Memo (14-Feb-2026)

Key News Highlights

  • US-Iran Escalation: The US military is preparing for potentially prolonged operations against Iranian-linked targets. Both diplomatic and military channels remain active, but the risk of direct conflict or expanded Middle East disruptions is notably higher.

  • Japan/China Tensions: Japanese authorities seized a Chinese fishing vessel, raising friction in East Asia.

  • Public Health: The US is seeing a sharp rise in measles cases, and the WHO has criticized the ethics of some US-backed vaccine trials.

Macro & Rates

  • Central Bank Outlook
    • The probability of a US recession by end 2026 remains moderate but not dominant.
    • Fed funds rate is being priced for a gradual but uneven set of cuts this year, with Treasury markets reflecting only modest easing expectations (FF ~3.6% as of latest FRED).
    • Market assigns little chance to a sudden crisis-driven cut; “higher for longer” remains plausible, particularly if Middle East disruption persists or inflation proves sticky.
  • Inflation & Data Dependency
    • FRED shows core CPI and PCE inflation decelerating but still running warm; sticky services/energy inputs are a risk if crude spikes.
    • Next catalysts: January US CPI (~2/20) and PCE data (~2/28). Markets may react to any upside surprise.
  • Yield Curve
    • Yield curve steepening slightly (10y-2y spread ~0.6–0.7%) and not inverted; financial stress measures remain subdued (Chicago Fed index).
  • USD & FX
    • USD broadly stable but at the high end of multi-year range. Geopolitical stress or a dovish pivot from other G10 central banks could drive vol/risk reversals.

Sectors

    • Insurance and reinsurance remain exposed to rising natcat/war premium, given elevated bets on record temperatures and increased natural disaster probabilities.

  • Tech, Semiconductors & AI

  • Financials
    • US large banks well-contained—major bank failure/downgrade risks are not imminent but longer-term fiscal risk and credit cycle deterioration into 2027 remain tail risks.
    • Housing finance/mortgage rates remain elevated (30Y ~6.2–6.4%), capping Mortgage REIT upside; delayed GSE (Fannie/Freddie) IPOs confirm sector malaise.

  • Consumer/Healthcare
    • Measles outbreak and public vaccine skepticism risk impacts for school, leisure, and travel equities.
    • Obesity and GLP-1 pipeline catalysts remain a factor after strong FDA/biopharma-driven performance.

  • Emerging Markets

Crypto & Alternatives

Risks & 2nd/3rd Order

  • US political gridlock, shutdown risk, and Fed leadership transition remain latent volatility sources.

  • Pandemic risk: New variants or outbreaks may reprice travel/leisure, select consumer retail, and reinsurance, but not a base case.
