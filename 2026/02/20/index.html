Macro/Markets

Geopolitics & Risk-Off Drivers

  • Middle East Escalation

    • Market-implied odds for US or Israel striking Iran before March 31 are now elevated (>50%); Iran closing the Strait of Hormuz risk also moving higher.
      • Oil is reflecting a significant risk premium, but not yet full "hot war" scenarios.
      • Immediate supply chain (shipping/insurance), energy, and select defense stocks are bid up.
      • The risk of gold and broad commodities surge if escalation continues; hedging via exposure to gold and select energy names justified.
    • Secondary global impact would weigh negatively on cyclicals (broad equities) and further pressure airlines, global trade routes, and insurance.

  • Russia/Ukraine & Europe

    • Odds favor continued conflict in Ukraine throughout 2026 (low ceasefire probability); US/EU troop intervention risk remains low, but equity risk premium in Europe persists.
    • Mild but persistent upward drift on wheat/energy-linked EM assets is possible.

  • US-China Trade/Technology

    • High probablity for US-China economic/trade confrontation; tariffs on China, Europe, Canada, and Mexico are nearly certain to rise in H2 2026.
      • Trade policy and regulatory risk remain major headwinds for US multinationals (tech, autos, semiconductors), especially those exposed to CNY/JPY.

Domestic Macro

  • Labor Market

    • UAW win in Tennessee/Volkswagen: labor wage pressures continue, likely catalyst for consumer inflation stickiness and margin pressure in manufacturing/auto.

  • Housing

    • Home price indices mixed but decelerating; new lending and housing starts stable but off prior highs.
    • No clear evidence of housing market distress, but prolonged high rates would threaten MBS/REIT valuations (especially if FOMC stays on hold longer than expected).
