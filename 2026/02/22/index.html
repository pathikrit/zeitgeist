Macroeconomic & Market Overview

Growth/Inflation

  • FRED/Actuals: Monthly core CPI and PCE inflation remain in the ~0.3% range. Year-over-year core metrics remain above Fed comfort zone, with sticky services inflation.
  • Labor market: Unemployment at a slightly elevated 4.3%; claims rolling higher, job openings (JOLTS) weakening but not collapsed. Employment data supports a "cooling but not recessionary" US labor market.
  • Prediction markets:
    • Market participants expect US GDP growth >2.5% in 2026, but probability is down from previous years. Macro risk of below-trend growth is material should layoffs accelerate.
    • Limited chance of negative GDP or high unemployment in 2026, though a >5% unemployment probability is rising.
  • Equities:
    • S&P 500 and Nasdaq-100 have moderate positive finish odds for 2026; range-bound outlook is dominant, with both up and downside "tails."
    • Price action this week likely range-bound, with snowstorm and travel disruption a near-term drag for discretionary/retail.

Interest Rates/Bonds

  • Yield curve: FRED shows a modestly positive 10Y–2Y and 10Y–3M spread (~+0.6%), consistent with "normalized" rather than recessionary forward curve.
  • Treasuries: Prediction markets expect 10Y yields to stay near 4–4.5% through year-end. Rate cut cycle likely to begin mid-2026, with cuts potentially slow. No near-term prospects for zero or negative rates.
  • Fed:
    • Stable bias in upcoming meetings; markets expect no surprise rate hikes or emergency cuts.
    • Nomination and likely confirmation of Kevin Warsh as Fed Chair is increasingly priced (expected by May). Warsh is seen as market-friendly but prone to “overshoot” in hawkish rhetoric.
  • Second-order impact: Elevated deficits, government shutdown noise, and sticky real rates keep pressure on long-duration Treasuries, favoring barbell strategies and TIPS over long-dated nominals.

Fiscal/Political Risk

  • US debt/debt ceiling:
    • Prediction markets assign a modestly rising risk to US sovereign downgrade and long-term debt crossing $40T before 2027.
    • Multiple government shutdowns and a lengthy DHS shutdown are priced in; the current impasse could persist through March. Real risk of a short-term drag on defense, government contractors, and GSE-funded MBS/REITs.
  • Regulation/tariffs:
    • 10% blanket US tariff is assumed to go into effect imminently; secondary legislative activity on additional tariffs or Congressional pushback (lawsuits, filibuster) expected but may be mostly procedural in the near term.
    • Any new tariffs will further pressure discretionary retail and global supply chains, raising inflation tail risk heading into Q2.
