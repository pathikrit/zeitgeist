Daily Memo (15-Feb-2026)

Reports of deteriorating conditions and ongoing offensives continue. Macro Backdrop & Thematic Risks

Rates, Inflation, and Federal Reserve

  • Market Signals

    • Core CPI and PCE prints remain elevated (Core CPI Jan: 332.8 vs. 331.8 in Dec; trend is gradual but not receding).
    • Unemployment (Jan: 4.3%) stabilizing near post-COVID cyclical lows, with payrolls growth still positive.
    • 10yr-2yr and 10yr-3m spreads remain with positive but declining slopes (e.g., 10Y: ~4.09%, 2Y: ~3.47%), indicative of a steepening bias but not fully normal.
    • Continuing claims are off recent highs, but still seasonally elevated.

  • Outlook

    • Virtually no near-term Fed action expected; FOMC guidance and market consensus centers on a prolonged hold at current levels, with rate cuts expected to phase in Q2–Q3 if labor softens or inflation cools.
    • Repricing risk if jobs or inflation data surprise to the upside (upcoming catalysts: March CPI and March FOMC).
    • Yield curve and financial conditions remain supportive of a soft landing view, but downside risks if CPI or jobs spike.

Fiscal, Political, and Legislative Uncertainty

  • 2027 Budget Season (starts March–April): Risks around appropriations and potential shutdowns will increase. Strong partisan divisions and highly visible fiscal fights heading into late spring.
  • Tariffs/Trade: Ongoing review of China tariffs by the administration with bipartisan pressure for more aggressive action by mid-2026—potential for incremental tariffs or enforcement headlines.
  • Debt Ceiling & US Ratings: With peak US debt projections sweeping through $40T before 2027, another downgrade/highlighted standoff is likely by year-end or early 2027—Treasuries, FX, and volatility risks rise if negotiation turns disruptive.

Geopolitics & Energy

  • Middle East/Iran: Harsher line from US/Israel on Iran (oil, uranium, possible kinetic action) increases probability of a regional oil/military shock; gold, crude, defense names, and volatility bid if rhetoric escalates or action is initiated.
  • Ukraine Conflict: Stalemate remains, but any breakthrough or sudden collapse/change (especially after the US security guarantee talk) could shift energy and defense sector outlooks significantly.
  • Far East (China/Taiwan/Japan):
    • China—US tension persists; incursion/kinetic action risk (Taiwan blockade or worse) is moderate; supply chain, semis, EM FX at risk if rhetoric turns aggressive.
    • JPY and Japan equities remain exposed to BOJ normalization and regional volatility.

Sector & Asset-Class Outlook

Equities

  • Broad US Indices

    • S&P 500 (SPX) risk/reward is balanced; the uptrend is fading (market internals weakening), while downside volatility jumps if labor or inflation miss. Earnings growth expected to be modest in Q1–Q2.
    • NASDAQ-100 (NDX) leadership remains with big tech and AI, but rotation to cyclicals or defensive (utilities, healthcare) themes is picking up.
    • Short-term market circuit breaker or event risk (e.g., government shutdown, Middle East kinetic) remains below baseline but not ruled out in H1.

  • Financials

    • Large bank CEO pay, despite elevated regulatory chatter, points to sector confidence. However, the next round of major bank earnings (April) and Q1 13F filings will reveal if loan growth and net interest margins are turning.
    • Major US bank bailout risk (still ~40% implied) is not immediate but is a lingering worry for sub-investment grade financials.
    • Credit card cap and potential regulatory rumblings could impact consumer/rate-sensitive lenders later in the year.

  • Tech/Semiconductors/AI

    • Nvidia remains the world’s largest by mkt cap (major support for hardware/AI supply chain, cloud spend, and next GTC as a trading event).
    • Critical Cloudflare, AWS, ChatGPT outages would reinforce tailwinds to diversified and resilient cloud providers; downside event risk to over-concentrated cloud holdings.
    • Further AI regulatory debate and chatter continue but US direct intervention (e.g., equity stakes or backstop) unlikely near-term.
    • Multiple high-profile tech IPOs (OpenAI, SpaceX, Anthropic) slotted between Q2–Q4; valuation expectations remain stretched.

  • Energy/Commodities

    • Real potential for sharp repricing in crude, gold, and safe-haven assets on Middle East escalation. OPEC+ meeting in March is key for oil direction; US rhetoric on Iran and enforcement on China-bound oil is a near-term catalyst.
    • Uranium, copper, and rare earths benefit from both geopolitical risk and US/EU industrial/fiscal push; next subsidy and grant announcements will offer directional cues.

  • Industrials, Defense

    • Geopolitical uncertainty (Ukraine, Iran/Israel, North Korea) continues to drive demand and order visibility for aerospace and defense primes.
    • Anduril and Lockheed Martin remain in strategic focus for federal procurement/discussions around government equity stakes, as do major airframe contractors like Boeing.
    • Boeing deliveries and order flow remain strong.

  • Healthcare/Biotech

    • Healthcare law and insurance credit headwinds are locked in for debate season (March–April; Supreme Court cases pending).
    • Infectious disease headlines (measles surge, WHO on vaccine trial ethics) are an overhang for select pharma and MCO names; Medicaid redeterminations and cost trend concern for hospitals persist.

  • Consumer

    • Retail sales (stable, slight YoY gains in Dec/Jan) and sentiment remain positive but not accelerating. Any labor market crack will impair discretionary, but home improvement (HD, LOW) remains resilient.
    • AI/robo-advice and fraud risk themes are growing for fintech, consumer, and cyber providers.

Crypto / Digital Assets

  • Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana

    • Spot ETF inflows neutral to slightly positive; positioning chatter leans bullish, but implied swings are increasing and tail risk is bi-directional post-halving.
    • Regulatory updates and Congressional rhetoric are picking up; US government equity/staking (e.g. in OpenAI, crypto exchanges, or token launches) is on the table for later in the year.

  • Event-Driven

    • Multiple major crypto token launches and IPOs this year (Paradex, Hyperliquid, MetaMask, OpenSea) likely to provide waves of marketcap and narrative volatility.
    • Potential for a major hack, stablecoin depeg, or regulatory headline remains elevated.

Second- & Third-Order Effects

  • Prolonged Middle East tension and hard-energy stance risk both a jump in headline inflation and sharp reversal in global risk assets; will put upward pressure on breakevens and deteriorate Fed cutting expectations if sustained.
  • US fiscal/political theater increases not just shutdown probability, but supply pressure on Treasuries—flat to upward bias for long-duration yields (esp. if auction demand is weak).
  • Any initiative to take government equity in strategic tech, semis, or defense AI (or “nationalize” certain assets) changes risk-reward for public suppliers—may drive ‘winner take most’, reduce small-cap competition, and increase regulatory premiums.
  • A growing drumbeat around AI fraud/cyber exposure and consumer data liability is raising both insurance and compliance costs for fintech, retail, and adtech—margin drag risk is non-trivial for those with weak controls.
  • Upside for spot crypto asset prices remains, but event-driven risks from regulatory, hacking, or key-person outcomes can cause major, short-term dislocations.
