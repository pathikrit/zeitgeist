Thematic & Sector Highlights

  • Energy & Materials

    • Crude oil, LNG, and critical minerals top the global equity winners; momentum in US shale/rig activity (per pred. mkt. consensus on high US oil output).
    • Persistent US policy on critical minerals, permitting reform, and stockpiling likely to channel flows into North American mining and infrastructure.
    • Insurers/reinsurers face claims in several directions: Mideast, natural disasters, climate, and potentially further pandemic surprise risk.

  • Tech & AI

    • IPO momentum: SpaceX IPO filing will drive renewed private-to-public capital flow in growth/AI hardware. Secondary effect: lifts tech M&A multiples, boosts risk premium for cloud/AI infrastructure suppliers (NVDA, AMD, TSMC, ARM).
    • GLP-1 catalyst: With ELI Lilly’s oral approval, large pharma (and specialty retail, insurer) pricing power shifts upward, but also pressurizes some healthcare suppliers and public insurers.
    • AI lawsuits & model risk: Ongoing copyright and safety litigation (OpenAI/Anthropic) increases regulatory tail risk for cloud/metaverse, but also demonstrates pricing power as cost of AI infrastructure moves up (utility cost/compute price inflation showing up).
    • Prediction markets assign high likelihood to further layoffs in tech, especially sectors exposed to consumer and SaaS.
    • Expect hyperscalers’ Q1 earnings to reveal double-digit cloud cost growth—model demand is outpacing hardware supply.

  • Autos/EV

    • Tesla deliveries expected to be robust in Q1, but with rising inventory and falling average selling prices (per markets/polls).
    • Weakness continues in secondary auto demand (CPI used vehicles staying flat/down); headwinds for legacy OEMs remain.

  • Financials

    • Bank earnings will be highly sensitive to credit risk, balance sheet funding cost (following rapid increase in short-end rates and tighter financial conditions).
    • Regional/consumer banks remain at risk of elevated nonperforming loans and MBS mark-to-market pressure.
    • Major bank/asset manager IPO and M&A activity signals more market breadth than prior quarters.

  • REITs/Housing

    • Median nominal US home price indices still drifting up (per FRED, pred. mkt. range). However, home transaction volumes (especially mid-high end) are mostly forecasted to slow, reflecting affordability headwinds and 30y mortgage rates solidly above 6.5%.
    • Legislation restricting single-family institutional buying is expected; REIT business models may require re-rating.

  • Crypto & Alternatives

    • Crypto active, second-order themes: US regulatory actions, ETF flows, and higher volatility indexes forecast for BTC and ETH.
    • Bitcoin and Ethereum ETF flows are currently flat to slightly negative; high realized volatility but no imminent shock from stablecoin peg risk.
    • Crypto fund insolvency and CEX insolvency risk linger but not yet acute.
    • Major new AI coins, token launches, and airdrops scheduled—expect sharp sector rotation and high retail engagement, as well as greater regulatory scrutiny.
