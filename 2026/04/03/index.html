Macro: Growth, Inflation, & Policy

US Macro and Rates

  • Inflation Trends:

    • Core CPI and Core PCE continue grinding higher in recent FRED prints (Core PCE Jan: 128.4); headline CPI due April 15.
    • Forward inflation expectations still trending above 2%, pricing in persistent, sticky inflation; prediction markets see inflation above 3.5% for the year as a baseline.
    • Oil and gas price spikes from Gulf attacks increase risk that April/May CPI prints will overshoot, compounding Fed hawkishness and margin pressure across sectors.

  • Labor Market:

    • Unemployment rate (Feb: 4.4%) remains well above the lows, with jobless claims ticking up and JOLTS continuing lower.
    • Majority expect high-profile layoff announcements (white-collar, tech, more than prior year), which, coupled with elevated bankruptcies, signals an ongoing shakeout.

  • Fed/Monetary Policy:

    • Prediction markets overwhelmingly expect the Fed to hold steady in April; June and out sees moderate probability of the first rate cut (50–60% by late summer).
    • However, markets price in a continued restrictive stance with year-end Fed funds >3.5%, and little belief in preemptive emergency cuts despite headline risks—reflecting strong "higher for longer" consensus.
    • Consensus coalescing around Kevin Warsh as imminent nominee for Fed Chair, solidifying the market's hawkish read on the next Fed regime.
    • Treasury curve: 10y at 4.33%, 2/10 still positively sloped, risk of further bear steepening if oil/war risk flows through to inflation and sovereign premiums.

  • Government Spending & Fiscal:

    • Prediction markets assign high probability to further fiscal gridlock, additional shutdowns, and baseline scenario of a rising deficit well above 5% of GDP.
    • GOP signaling plans for single-topic appropriations (ICE, Defense); durable gridlock likely, risk of further downgrades, and negative implications for Treasuries.
