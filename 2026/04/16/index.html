Macro & Markets

US Macro

  • Soft-landing consensus holding but some risk skew toward inflation persistence—recent FRED data show core CPI continuing to rise through March, with headline inflation (retail, food, energy) in the headlines and monthly payroll growth mostly flat. Unemployment steady around the mid-4s.
  • The Fed remains on pause; the market assigns little immediate probability to a rate change at the late-April FOMC, but expects possible cuts in Q3/Q4. Dissent risk rising within the Fed.

Rates & Credit

  • Treasury curve: marginally positive slope (~50bps 10y–2y), with long-end yields elevated (10y at 4.26%). Financial conditions remain loose, but spreads are not signaling imminent recession. Supply risk prominent: May refunding and auction schedule is a near-term risk.
  • High uncertainty persists for credit: bankruptcy risk remains a topic (esp. junk credits) but not acute in systemics. Fed balance sheet flatlining above $6T.

FX

  • USD index weakens on improved truce prospects and retreating haven demand, but expected to remain volatile pending Middle East and trade headlines.
  • EUR/USD, USD/JPY anticipated most reactive to G7/ECB/BOJ communication as FX market attention shifts back to central bank policies if Mideast risks subside.

Commodities

  • Oil: WTI retraced from $115+ to ~$100, but energy market remains tight. Prolonged Hormuz disruption would swiftly tighten global supply, reprice inflation, and shock risk assets.
  • Ag: Fertilizer, ground beef, retail food CPI on rise—consumer staples and input-cost risk for food and CPG equities.

Geopolitics

  • Live Middle East situation: any expansion of the conflict, or failure of ceasefire talks, would drive energy, defense, and gold sharply higher.
