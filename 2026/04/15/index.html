Daily Memo (15-Apr-2026)

Top News

  • US-Iran tension remains high: The US says the Hormuz blockade is fully implemented, turning back Iranian tankers, but is signaling openness to new negotiations. Hopes for an imminent peace deal have tempered, but base case is status quo for now. Oil and shipping names remain headline-sensitive.
  • Asia and global equity rally: Asian shares are up, powered by reduced war risks and tech optimism. US (especially tech) equities continue to show resilience, with rotation into rate- and cyclically-sensitive sectors on US-Iran de-escalation.
  • Corporate earnings in focus: Large US banks and big tech report over the next 2–3 weeks, setting key tone for risk. Continued strong data center/AI demand is expected to lift cloud and semiconductor results.
  • Job market and inflation: US jobless claims remain contained; no recent spike, unemployment trending just above 4%. March core CPI rose sequentially, keeping sticky inflation and delayed Fed rate cuts in play.
  • Layoffs and white-collar pressure: Ongoing and anticipated layoffs at Snap and major tech firms. Workforce rightsizing in the tech sector is not abating—sentiment negative for hiring-sensitive software and fintech names.

Macro & Rates

  • US growth and labor: Monthly jobs added (Mar: +178k), labor force steady, and JOLTS job openings remain subdued but not collapsing. The FRED data shows modest labor cooling but no outright contraction.
  • Inflation, rates and policy: March core CPI and PCE continue to grind higher (CPI core index: 334.2). Headline inflation is sticky in the 3.5–4% range, delaying any Fed easing. Futures pricing/markets indicate stasis through first half; rate-cut timing and magnitude now expected much later in the year (if at all).
  • Yield curve & USD: Modest yield curve steepening, but still flat (10Y–2Y spread: 0.52%). 10Y yield steady near 4.3%. Dollar index drifting lower with de-escalation and higher risk appetite, but no broad dollar break yet.

Market Risks/Catalysts

  • Fiscal/policy backdrop: Government shutdown risk has faded for now, but major fiscal issues/US debt ceiling deadlines develop in coming months.
  • Geopolitics/war premium: US-Iran risk is a crucial driver for crude, shipping, and defense sectors. War escalation risk is reduced (war "close to over" per administration), but news-driven volatility will persist until formal agreement or situational change.
  • Fed leadership: Warsh apparent to replace Powell imminently—no indication of radical shift yet, but any surprise in opening remarks could jolt rates/financials.

Sectors

Energy & Commodities

  • Oil: WTI oil ~114/bbl, up sharply over the last two weeks. Markets have priced in some risk premium from Hormuz actions but have not (yet) priced in full supply disruption. De-escalation headlines could pull crude back, but upside risk remains from further conflict escalation.
  • Shipping: Iranian blockade is crimping global oil shipping. Tankers/port logistics exposed, with second- and third-order impacts to global supply chains.
  • Gold/silver: Precious metals bid on geopolitical tension and inflation staying elevated. Silver outperformed last week, and prediction markets signal high ongoing realized volatility.
  • Agriculture: No major movement yet, but watch weather and fertilizer price signals for Q2 ag flows.

Technology & AI

  • AI leadership shift: Betting markets now consistently favor Anthropic over OpenAI for both product leadership and IPO timing. New model launches/benchmarks expected soon. AI sector remains risk-on; upside skew for supplier names (NVDA, cloud, LLM platforms).
  • Tech layoffs: Cycle not over. Heightened risk for mid-cap and large non-leader tech. Perpetuating labor surplus will put further downward pressure on wages and engineers’ comp multiples; possible hiring leverage for platform leaders.

Financials

  • Banks/Earnings: Large cap banks beat expectations in early earnings (esp. trading/IB), but regional bank conditions remain a focal point. Stress in commercial real estate portfolios and NIM compression are emerging into rotation out of regional/riskier banks.
  • Credit: Corporate defaults are rising but not spiking; risk is skewed toward speculative grade and asset-light sectors.

Consumer & Housing

  • Housing affordability stress: Mortgage rates remain elevated (~6.4–6.5%), Case-Shiller and starts show stabilization but no fresh rebound. Single-family REITs likely to face more scrutiny as legislative risk rises (institutional investment restrictions likely this year).
  • Retail: March retail sales show ongoing strength, but channel checks suggest discretionary names are most exposed to downside, especially if war premium pushes gas or food costs higher into May.

Industrials & Cyclicals

  • PMI/production: Production up, but not surging. ISM and durable goods remain neutral, benefitting select names most exposed to defense/wartime procurement and semicap supply chain.

Crypto

In summary:
Risk premium for war remains but is drifting lower barring escalation; oil remains headline/volatility-driven. Fed is firmly on hold through spring, with sticky inflation and job market resilience putting soft landing or "stagflation-lite" as the consensus base case. AI/semicaps lead tech structurally; labor market news and Q2/Q3 earnings are the next catalysts. Geopolitical and shutdown/debt ceiling remain medium-horizon tail risks. Watch for softening in consumer and white-collar cyclicals. Stay tactical on defense, oil, and Big Tech into key catalysts.
