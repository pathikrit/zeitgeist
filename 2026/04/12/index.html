  • Earnings & Corporate Headlines

  • Political & Geopolitical

    • Foreign opposition and palace intrigue in China and Iran continue.

Macro/Markets

  • Inflation & Rates

    • March core CPI and PCE continue to grind higher. Headline CPI jump (oil spike) is filtering into consumer goods. FRED: core CPI up another 0.2-0.3% mom, headline expected higher on April report.
    • Short end (2Y) yields at ~3.8%, 10Y at ~4.3%. Yield curve remains modestly positive. Street increasingly bears out higher-for-longer consensus—little sign of a pivot given sticky inflation and war premium.
    • USD TWI inches up on risk aversion and yield support.

  • Employment, Real Economy

    • Labor market showing resilience—March payrolls, jobless claims, and unemployment hanging within the 4.3–4.4% range. Openings still >6.8M per FRED.
    • Consumer sentiment hovers at low 50s; risk of sentiment rollover if gasoline/food inflation persists.
    • Retail sales and industrial production stable but no reacceleration.

  • Fiscal Policy

    • Multiple government shutdowns remain a possibility—fiscal deal/budget deadlines are live and gridlock risk is high.
    • Ongoing risk around debt ceiling and government funding. Massive supply of Treasuries to fund persistent deficit (FRED M2 up, but so is Treasury issuance).

Key Themes / 2nd/3rd Order Impacts

War & Commodity Volatility

  • Oil

    • WTI spot surges in response to Hormuz risk; above $110 and holding. Option-implieds highlight potential for $120–$150 test if war escalates again or Hormuz transit clogs.
    • Elevated gasoline/diesel prices filtering into CPI, PPI, and consumer sentiment. Headwinds for travel, consumer cyclicals, retail.
    • $USD strength serves as partial release valve—energy equities, oil & gas, pipelines, and select exporters benefit, but EM/Europe risk off.

  • Shipping

    • Mining of Hormuz and insurance spikes create bottlenecks—logistics/commodity inflation will stay sticky even if mines cleared short term. Risks to industrial/materials/exporters.

  • Gold/Silver/Safe Haven

    • Gold and silver making new highs on geopolitical risk and inflation surprises. Flows into monetary metals and TIPS as stagflation hedge.

US Macro Path and the Showdown on Soft Landing

  • Sticky Inflation

    • Consensus shifting: soft landing hopes challenged by inflation persistence (energy, rents, food). With war premium in commodities, and labor market staying tight, little room for multiple near-term Fed cuts.
    • Expect CPI and PPI prints in coming weeks to remain elevated given energy pass-throughs—upside risk to TIPS/bond market volatility.

  • Yield Curve

    • Curve is fractionally positive for now, but risk is momentum to higher long-end yields (reflation, risk premium) if inflation sticks and deficit remains untamed.

  • Deficit & Rates

    • Large issuance needs, sticky inflation, and no deficit improvement steer real yields higher. Second order: headwinds for high-duration growth equities and stretched private markets.
    • Treasuries & TIPS: demand at risk if foreign buyers turn cautious due to USD rates, credit concerns, or domestic absorption.

Sector & Asset Class Impacts

  • Energy / Oil Majors / Refiners

    • Positive torque to cash flows, but political risk (windfall taxes, sanctions) rises as prices spike. Service co’s (e.g. HAL) gaining margin.

  • Travel / Airlines / REITs

    • Airfare and hotel sectors face margin risk as costs jump and travel sentiment wanes (TSA volumes with potential to soften). Insurance/REITs exposed to energy and hurricane risk with early warning signals in climate data.

  • Big Tech / AI / Semis

    • AI capex and hyperscaler earnings theme is front and center (Nvidia, Big 5 earnings approaching). Persistent AI infra demand, but some PC hardware names (Framework) flag risk of demand cannibalization in classic segments.
    • Regulatory and security blowback cycle in AI as sector draws scrutiny. OpenAI incident heightens litigation and tail risk.

  • Autos / EVs

    • Commodity pressures squeeze auto/EV input margins. Still, US/European automakers with US supply chains relatively better positioned near term.

  • Defense

    • Defense, contractors, and aerospace (SpaceX, Anduril, LMT, etc.) see positive order momentum; any signs of war de-escalation could hit valuations, but for now, procurement tailwinds remain large.

  • Biotech / Healthcare

    • No short-term catalyst, but Supreme Court and legislative risk loom heading into summer—Medicare, drug pricing, and monopolization bills in play.

  • Financials / Banks

    • Spreads are adequate, credit remains stable, but market pricing in stepped up default risk at “stress” names. No systemic risk at large banks but regionals still on watchlist.
    • Treasury and mortgage spread volatility = select weakness in housing, REITs, and lending franchises.

  • Crypto

    • High volatility and ETF flow sensitivity tied to moves in rates, risk appetite, and regulatory risk—short-term rangebound but event risk remains high.

Country/Political Risk

  • China/Taiwan/EM
    • No immediate invasion premium, but tail risks remain non-trivial. Ongoing domestic political churn (Xi leadership, opposition meetings) could surprise markets. CNY/China equities remain out of favor.
  • Europe
    • France/Hungary/Peru all provide EM or political risk setups. ECB and BOE both under pressure to stay hawkish as inflation beats. EUR/USD not expected to strengthen near term, risk-off tone continues.
