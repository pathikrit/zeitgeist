Key News Items

  • US–Iran Ceasefire: A two-week ceasefire has been reached; both sides are claiming victory. Trump’s rhetoric remains provocative, leading to calls for his removal by over 85 lawmakers. Pope Leo issued a strong rebuke of Trump’s threat to destroy “a whole civilization.”
  • Global Markets Rally on Ceasefire: U.S. equities are bid higher; Dow futures surged and oil prices declined sharply after the ceasefire. Treasuries and risk assets are responding positively.
  • Fed, ECB, BoJ in Focus: Discussion of rates and future policy, with increasing market expectations that the Fed will maintain current policy in April and June. Treasury and BoJ yields are stable, EURUSD trading steady ahead of policy meetings.
  • AI Security Highlighted: Anthropic announced Project Glasswing, highlighting AI’s dual-use risk in both cyberdefense and cyberattacks. Claude Mythos model flagged significant vulnerabilities in major systems.
  • Geopolitics: North Korea missile launches, tense US–Iran, US–China, and EU–MENA dynamics. Several European leaders expressing growing concerns about strongman durability and democratic backsliding in Hungary.
  • March FRED Data: Core CPI and PCE inflation remain elevated (Core CPI at 333.5 as of Feb, unemployment holding at 4.3% as of March, jobless claims and labor market tread water); 10yr UST yield holding ~4.34%.

Macro & Rates

  • Inflation: CPI/PPI remain sticky; FRED Core CPI prints show ongoing upward pressure into early 2026. Major inflation prints due this week—expectation for headline and core remain above 3% y/y, supporting the higher-for-longer narrative.
    • Short-run: Market pricing is removing any probability of imminent Fed rate cuts in April. Longer-dated contracts price cuts more mid/late-year as growth slows, but second-half inflation risk skew remains notable.
    • Bond yields: 10Y UST ~4.34%, 2Y ~3.84%, curve modestly positive in 2y/10y. Credit spreads narrowed post-ceasefire, but funding markets not signaling stress.
  • Labor Market: Payrolls continue to show sluggish growth, but not outright contraction. Continuing claims remain steady, no sharp layoffs outside tech sector.
  • Global Macro: Commodity and FX markets are reacting to geopolitics (oil down, USD steady). DXY remains firm (~120.5), risk-on tone in EM FX.

Geopolitics & Event Risk

  • US–Iran: The ceasefire sharply reduces immediate oil market tail risk; WTI fell >$10/bbl. However, the political tone and GOP–Dem splits highlight that risk premium may re-emerge.
  • US–China: Upcoming high-level trade talks; tariffs and technology controls to dominate, with semiconductors (NVDA, TSMC, ASML) and EV supply chain (TSLA, BYD, lithium/copper names) exposure at stake.
  • Europe: ECB expected on hold, but inflation surprises or risk-premium repricing (Italy or Hungary political risks) can spill into rates/banks. Hungary’s upcoming elections now seen as a potential inflection for EU integration/risk premia.
  • Russia/Ukraine: Ceasefire in Ukraine holding, though underlying risk of renewed conflict not eliminated—keep watching energy, ag, and major defense sector positioning.

Sectors & Thematic Notes
