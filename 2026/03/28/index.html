Macro Rundown

  • Yield Curve & Fed: 2Y at 3.96%, 10Y at 4.42%; modestly positive 10Y–2Y spread but no signal of imminent steepening. Fed Funds steady near 3.6%. Expectations remain for extended higher policy rates—consensus for FOMC to stay put late-April, but multiple dissents likely, reflecting internal Fed splits on inflation.
  • Core Inflation: Core CPI and PCE readings trend higher; FRED’s latest points to stubbornly sticky core prints above 3%. Market-implied probabilities have sharply reduced the odds of spring/summer rate cuts, with only a mild pickup by late 2026.
  • DHS Shutdown/Spending: Funding impasse lasting >40 days; market pricing reflects modest tail risk to confidence and possible downgrade chatter. Debt ceiling/debt sustainability will return to front burner 2H26—expect higher Treasury volatility and a steepening bias if Congressional dysfunction persists.

Real Economy

  • Jobs & Labor: Payrolls show stagnation (flat net gains, per FRED Feb), unemployment running 4.4–4.5% (drifting up since 2025). Continuing claims remain elevated but stable—labor market is cooling, not freefalling, but job destruction headlines and Challenger cuts are rising, especially in tech.
  • Consumer & Housing: Consumer sentiment remains depressed near cyclical lows (U. Mich ~56). Retail sales flat; home prices grinding higher but at a slower pace, while housing starts are rebounding modestly. Mortgage rates remain stuck above 6.5%; REIT fundamentals at risk from new anti-institutional investment regulation likely in 2026.
  • Inflation Trends: Headline and core CPI up MoM, exceeding FOMC targets (FRED core CPI: +0.7% and sticky at 3%+ YoY). CPI and PPI, especially energy and food components, are set to accelerate again next month given oil shock.
  • Trade/FX: USD stays firm but off the highs, trade-weighted index at 120. Key yield differentials to Europe/Japan maintain USD advantage; trade deficit set for new highs as energy imports spike. No meaningful improvement in the current account outlook.

Commodities

Nat Gas spot price volatility also elevated due to disrupted LNG flows.

Geopolitics & Sector Implications

  • Hormuz/Shipping: Iranian threats materialize; up to 80% of normal tanker traffic halted or at risk in Strait of Hormuz, per maritime trackers. Ensuing U.S./coalition response expected—watch for insurance cost surges and possible war-risk exclusion, raising costs for shippers and global supply chains. Oil, LNG shipping, and defense equities to benefit despite broader market turmoil.
  • EM/FX/CDS: Watch for stress in EM sovereigns with import dependency (India/China), as well as regionals (EUR, JPY) exposed to energy price pass-through.
  • Defense/Industrial Complex: Munition restocking risk—and structural underinvestment—are surfacing. U.S./NATO supply chains highly levered to Raytheon, Lockheed, Northrop, as Tomahawk and SAM inventory depletion may require emergency appropriations.

Domestic/Legislative

Even if TSA funding is temporarily restored, persistent Congressional dysfunction is likely to reprice sovereign and agency CDS wider this spring.
  • Big Tech/Regulation: Tech faces new legal headwinds (jury verdicts on child harm, antitrust signals), raising probability of platform liability, regulatory fines, and disclosure risk. Consensus for full-on ban on quarterly reporting still low, but incremental relaxation could bring more volatility to quarterly-driven trading.
