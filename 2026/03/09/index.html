Macro

Geopolitics, Oil/Energy

  • Iran war is now a direct, high-casualty, open conflict involving US and Israel.

    • Escalation risk to wider MENA region is acute and on a path-dependent trajectory; odds of US or Israeli strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities are nontrivial by late March/April.
    • Middle East/North Africa logistics, especially oil transit chokepoints, are at acute risk—Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed (>90% chance), global tanker insurance/flows disrupted.

  • Khamenei succession is not leading to near-term policy moderation—succession is dynastic, reducing collapse/regime change tail risk for now.

  • US foreign policy: Admin focus is broadening from tactical to regime change hopes—greater war duration/tail risk for defense, energy, export, and insurance sectors.

  • Domestic elections: Trump and GOP are using the war to drive calls for federal election oversight; increased state/federal standoffs and risks to legislative process.
