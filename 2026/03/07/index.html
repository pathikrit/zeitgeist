Macro: US & Global

US Macro & Rates

  • FRED and markets both signal persistent core inflation (Core CPI at 332.8 in Jan), with headline CPI holding above Fed’s 2% target. Current 10Y-2Y Treasury spread back in steep positive territory (~+60bp), reflecting market belief that “hard landing” risk is off for Q2 but not vanishing for year-end.
  • Consensus building for no FOMC rate cut in March (dot plot update and dissent risk higher than usual given policy uncertainty). April/June cuts possible but currently markets are discounting a “wait and see” stance.
  • Labor market cooling but not rolling over: Unemployment rate at 4.4% (Feb print), jobless claims stable, JOLTS openings have fallen to <6.55M as of December. FRED data reinforces message that tightening is now visible in hiring and churn, but not yet a crisis.
  • Sectors most sensitive to rates, labor cost, and retail trends: Energy, consumer discretionary, housing, and cyclicals. Mortgage rates still elevated, housing affordability/starts dampened.

US Policy & Political Uncertainty

  • Midterm risk remains asymmetric: Democratic hold on the House expected, Republican odds tightening for Senate. Markets are pricing in marginally less political volatility (credit spread steady, vol measures off highs), but an impeachment risk for Trump and potential for sudden power shifts persist.
  • Debt ceiling/funding, shutdown, and fiscal outlook: Short-term funding risk remains nontrivial but not imminent. Large government spending and national debt numbers are trending well above previous cycles — direct implication: yields will stay high, TIPS and long-duration treasuries remain volatile.
  • Major Supreme Court and regulatory decisions expected through mid-year with spillover into healthcare, student debt, tech, and energy.
  • Ongoing speculation and headlines around new rounds of tariffs (esp. major trading partners, pharma, minerals, semis), but legislative odds for direct consumer stimulus checks are still unfavorable.

Global Backdrop

  • US–Iran and Israel–Iran conflict now directly shaping energy and defense equity risk premium: risk of further oil infrastructure attacks (Strait of Hormuz closure probability remains highly elevated), regional shipping interruptions.
  • Risk of supply shock to oil (WTI now climbing, further OPEC+ tightening expected) plus gold and gas moving higher as risk offsets. European energy/defense equities remain a tail hedge.
  • China: Soft-landing hopes vs. slow-moving macro drag. Trump–Xi summit is now tightly linked to security matters, lowering probability any major tariff reduction or investment deal gets done until events stabilize.
  • Key global economic data: EU/UK/Japan inflation readings suggest central banks remain on pause, with mild easing bias. German politics and Eurozone peripheral risk heating up again (debt downgrade/sovereign index risk).
