Macro & Fed

The latest FRED print for Core CPI (Feb 2026: 333.51, up ~0.2% m/m) and fresh prediction market consensus point to full-year 2026 inflation sustaining above 3.5%, with upside risks amplified by oil shocks and supply disruptions. The narrative is solidly into “stagflation tail risk” territory.

U.S. Equities and Sector Themes

Market Structure & Risk

  • SPX/NQ: S&P 500 and Nasdaq both ran up through March; prediction markets now reflect deeply split risk, with an unusually wide 2026 range being priced (SPX: 6,000–7,600; NQ: 20,000–26,500 printed as realistic bands).

  • Leadership: Nvidia maintains role as world’s largest company by cap, but market tone implies mean reversion risk if U.S. tech earnings or regulation misfire in Q2.

Sector and Second-Order Implications

  • Energy: Direct beneficiary of sustained price increase, but supply/demand imbalances and potential for windfall taxes or price controls increase tail risks.
  • Industrials/Transports: Margin pressure likely due to fuel, input cost squeezes, and logistics bottlenecks (see ongoing shipping disruption at Hormuz/Suez).
  • Financials: Rates stuck higher for longer. Mortgage rates remain elevated (>6.5%), with further upward risk. Higher funding costs, increased default/credit risk, and regulatory scrutiny are going to squeeze regional/mid-tier banks.
  • REITs/Housing: Home price indices and housing starts are softening at the margin. Elevated mortgage rates, inflation, and high energy costs signal embedded downside risk for developers/REITs dependent on high leverage.
  • Tech & AI: AI bubble still intact, with sustained high capital investment and rapid model iteration. Apple, Alphabet, Meta all launching new features; regulation (SEC, EU, China) remains wild card. Downside for smaller SaaS, fintech, and frothy speculation increases if rates don’t budge or if credit spreads widen.
  • Consumer: Stagflation risk is real. Gasoline’s impact on lower-income discretionary spend will feed into Q2 retail and staples earnings.
  • Defense: U.S. and European defense equities are set for continued outperformance if war premium persists (see Israel’s new death penalty law, ongoing U.S./Israeli operations in several theaters).
