Macro

Multiple rate cuts are now generally expected through 2026, but policymakers remain cautious, referencing war risks and inflation uncertainties.

    • Recent FRED data: Nonfarm Payrolls have been treading water; unemployment ticked up to 4.3% in Jan (from 4.2% prior), in line with a cooling but not collapsing market; jobless claims drifted higher last month.
    • Upcoming payrolls releases (Apr, May) and next week’s retail sales will clarify if the soft patch worsens.

    • Impeachment/removal probabilities for Trump remain non-negligible; continued delay on fiscal appropriations for DHS amplifies confidence/risk premiums in credit (see shutdown risk below).

    • Consensus points to US government shutdown risk remaining elevated through March as appropriations remain gridlocked; protracted shutdown would have second-order effects on growth, sentiment, Treasuries, and dollar liquidity.

Geopolitics

Middle East Crisis Explodes

  • Impact on markets and portfolios:
    • Oil supply disruption is no longer tail risk: sustained strikes on Iran's missile and oil infrastructure—plus Iranian retaliation (including on Gulf state oil facilities)—introduce a broad risk-premium. WTI is already moving higher, with derivatives (options/skew) showing further aggressive upside pricing into Q2.
    • Defense and cybersecurity names benefit in the short term; possible re-rating of some insurance and shipping equities (risk of embargoes, war clauses triggered, higher reinsurance rates). Conflict could force risk-parity deleveraging.
  • Macro spillovers:
    • Hostilities risk closure/intermittent closure of both the Strait of Hormuz and Suez Canal, pushing up not just oil but LNG, fertilizer, and container shipping costs; pass-through into headline CPI will be closely watched.
    • FX: Strengthening USD as risk-off and real yields firm.
    • Possible renewed risk of an EM (incl. Turkish, Gulf, and North African) sovereign debt or FX event if conflict closes sea lanes.

Other Global Hotspots

  • Russia/Ukraine: Ukraine offensive action and cross-border activity continue, but overshadowed by Mideast crisis; German/Euro growth faces new downside from energy and security escalation.
  • Pakistan/Afghanistan: Local conflict intensifies; commodity (wheat/fertilizer) supply chains at risk.

US Macro/Sector

Equity Market Dynamics

  • Index outlook: S&P 500 technical downside levels in play (6,100–6,200); directional volatility to remain high until a ceasefire or credible war risk premium reduction.
  • Tech: Beta in mega-cap tech (NVDA, MSFT, AAPL) flips negative on risk-off episodes and possible regulatory action/antitrust activity both US and EU-side; AI infrastructure stocks face new overhang as DOD and Biden administration push government control or forced equity stakes.
  • Financials/Banks: US bank earnings in April are the next major event; risk of further deposit outflows and NIM compression if rate cuts are delayed. No imminent signs of a systemic credit event, but high yield spreads drifting wider.

Energy/Commodities

  • Oil: Realized supply shocks now front and center; OPEC+ ministerial remains a Q2 catalyst but market is no longer assuming coordinated price management, more defensive risk premium gets priced in.
  • Gold/Precious Metals: Gold rallies on both real yield drops and crisis hedge. Miners (GOLD, NEM) reflect tightening cost/power structures.
  • Industrial metals/utilities: Policy moves on critical minerals for security push up their value; pending US/EU stockpile legislation a key watch for metals/industrial ETF flows.

Housing/Real Estate

  • Mortgage rates: With 10yr yields at ~4.1%, 30yr mortgage rates expected to stay >6%. Case-Shiller Home Price Index is stable, but high rates, stickier inflation, and risk-off sentiment could drag down homebuilders and REITs.
  • **Legislation restricting institutional SFR remains a likely overhang for residential REITs in the medium term.

Healthcare/Pharma

  • Multiple states are re-emphasizing public health emergency responses amid spiraling measles cases; this could translate to renewed momentum for vaccine makers (MRNA, PFE), especially if credibility or hospital capacity is questioned.
  • Uncertain future for AI regulation in drug/diagnostics indicates no new major sector winners near-term; risk is concentrated on supply chain and pricing reform.

Technology & AI

  • AI Policy/M&A: The Pentagon’s pressure and legal wrangling on Anthropic—and risk of government-mandated equity in key AI vendors—injects existential uncertainty for growth investors in Anthropic, OpenAI, and second-tier cloud providers.
    • If escalation continues: watch for further US or EU steps to force onshoring of AI infrastructure, hard export controls on advanced chips, and reduced growth multiples on all AI/semiconductor trajectories.
  • AI model leadership is fragmented: Claude models (Anthropic) and OpenAI (ChatGPT) both compete for key market milestones, but regulatory and legal outcomes now can override technical progress.
  • IPO market: Tech and AI IPO window remains open but increasingly politicized; both SpaceX and OpenAI/Anthropic are in late-stage dialogue but timelines at risk given war and regulator posture.

Crypto & Alternatives

  • Crypto market structure: Bitcoin and major coins rally with the risk-off move in equities; upside risks accentuated in event of capital controls or new sovereign purchases (several EM nations rumored to shift further reserves into BTC).
  • ETFs and inflows: ETF flows show net inflows into Bitcoin and Ethereum ETF products, but highly sensitive to sharp rate or macro shocks.
  • Regulatory environment: Continued uncertainty on new US crypto regulation or capital gains reform; no new policy breakthroughs likely until post-shutdown.
