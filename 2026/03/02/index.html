Macro Environment

Geopolitics, Energy & Commodities

  • Iran Conflict: Second-Order Effects

    • Direct attacks on core oil infrastructure are a worst-case scenario for energy markets. Disruptions at Abqaiq, Safaniya, Kharg Island, and Ruwais are credible, with further risk to Hormuz traffic (global oil chokepoint).
      • WTI above $66–68 signals market pricing in sustained supply risk; OPEC+ is attempting to ramp up, but strategic inventories remain vulnerable.
    • Mass airspace closures and Gulf port disruptions are materially affecting shipping, supply chains, and global travel—negatively impacting airlines, tourism, and exporters, and propelling safe-haven flows.
    • The threat of further escalation—e.g., US/Israeli strikes on Iranian nuclear sites (Fordow, Natanz)—would deepen energy, defense, and gold upside risk, while adding broad downside tail risk to risk assets.
    • Iran’s leadership succession scenario (Khamenei) is now a focus; a power vacuum or regime collapse would ignite further instability with potentially regional contagion risk.

  • Domestic Security and Political Risk (US/Europe)

    • Heightened US security posture (DHS warnings) may cause persistent risk premiums for defense/security names, disrupt labor/consumer activity, and strain state/local budgets.
    • US/Europe "decoupling," as signaled by official commentary, points to longer-term divergence in industrial, energy, and defense policy; negative for EU cyclicals and multinational US equities with EME/African/MENA exposure.

  • Rates & Macro

    • Yield curve (10Y–2Y spread) remains positive but slightly steepening; FRED shows 10Y yields firming above 4%. The market is transitioning from pure recession fear to stagflation risk pricing (oil shock + regional war).
    • Fed is under pressure to weigh inflationary oil spike vs. growth drag from trade/travel/consumer disruption and confidence effects.

Inflation, Labor Markets & Growth

  • Inflation Trends

    • Core CPI continued its upward march (Jan 2026: 332.8), while core PCE is lagging but stable. Oil shocks will likely drive headline prints higher from March onward.
    • Predictive data shows headline/energy-driven YoY inflation breaching 3% is now plausible amid sustained input cost disruptions, with core sticky but less explosive.

  • Labor Market

    • US unemployment is ticking up—but only moderately (Jan: 4.3%). Payrolls are off highs; jobless claims (~1.8–1.9M) are not flashing crisis but could spike under prolonged travel/shipping disruptions or mass layoff events in affected sectors (airlines/travel/tourism).
    • Measles and public health events now present additional labor force disruption risk, especially for in-person service sectors.

  • Growth

    • GDP print risks are to the downside given real-time impact to travel, trade, and consumer behavior. FRED industrial production (+1.77% YoY last read) and retail sales are stable but face acute headwinds for March.
    • Sentiment (U. Mich: 56.4 in Jan) has not yet fully reflected war/pandemic spikes—expect sharp drops in coming readings, with further downstream implications for cyclical/reopening names.

Fixed Income, USD & Credit

  • Credit/Stress
    • Chicago Fed Financial Conditions Index remains easy (–0.56); financial markets have not "seized up" yet but stress could rise rapidly if Gulf shutdowns linger, or if there is a US federal shutdown (watch Spring 2026 funding negotiations).
    • Treasuries: Long bonds are bid but yields remain sticky >4% as stagflation risk sets in.
    • USD Index (117.99) remains firm; EM/Asia FX (esp. oil importers, tourism-dependent) are vulnerable if conflict escalates. Gold and oil are the direct volatility hedges.

Tech/AI

  • Macro-Driven Repricing: Expect further pressure on cyclicals, global trade, and manufacturing—especially those with direct Mid-East and EM exposure.

  • Maintain/raise allocations to energy, gold, defense, USD, and short duration fixed income.
  • AI/tech: overweight those with defense/government cyber and cloud exposure; underweight consumer-exposed, non-defensive SaaS.
  • Climate/alternative/critical materials: Chinese and European metals/minerals will see disruption upside pricing.
  • Healthcare: overweight defensives; pharma/biotech names focused on vaccine/communicable disease solutions.
