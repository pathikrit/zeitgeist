Daily Memo (11-Mar-2026)

  • Multiple Commercial Ships Attacked: Incidents in and near the Strait of Hormuz; some regional airports (Dubai) hit by drones; Red Sea and Gulf shipping risk continues to roil energy markets.

  • US Domestic Politics: Trump administration messaging on Iran grows inconsistent; White House unmoved (for now) by high oil, but pain threshold may be tested if volatility persists; DHS shutdown continues with potential for prolonged disruption.
  • Macro datapoints: Fed funds at ~3.6%, unemployment 4.4% (recently flat), jobless claims and labor market steady. Core CPI still climbing (Jan print: 332.8, about 2%+ YoY run rate).

1. Macro & Policy

U.S. Macro

  • Growth
    • FRED data and market indicators show softening but not recessionary activity: core CPI remains sticky above 3%, labor market steady, payrolls flat.
    • Retail sales and industrial output stable; no acute cyclical drag yet.
    • Market pricing has shifted to expect rate cuts not earlier than summer/fall, with high uncertainty near March FOMC.
  • Inflation
    • Prior months: persistent core inflation (core CPI, PCE keep climbing), commodities firming up.
    • Oil volatility and global shipping disruption are immediate transmission mechanisms for further CPI prints; second-order risk if disruptions persist and unwind value chains.
  • Rates
    • Yield curve remains positive, but not steep. 2Y at ~3.5%, 10Y at ~4.1–4.15%—supporting reasonable (but reduced) prospects for growth sectors.
    • High odds for at least one rate cut in H2 2026, but Fed’s near-term posture is on hold. Leadership transition risk is growing.
  • Fiscal Risk
    • US government shutdown (notably of DHS) ongoing; high risk of protracted disruption (>30, even >60 days possible).
    • Treasury issuance and debt levels continue to grow, but market signals (spreads, auctions) show little sign of panic—watch for QRA.

Geopolitics – Oil, Trade, & Defense Risk

  • Middle East/Supply Chain
    • Strait of Hormuz is a true chokepoint again: shipping attacks and mine threats pushed energy and insurance risk higher, with ripple effects through global cost structures and supply chains.
    • IEA reserve release could moderate price spikes, but risks of further production/supply losses remain elevated.
    • Direct US/Iran/Israel confrontation over next 1–2 months cannot be ruled out. Any miscalculation will directly impact global equities, energy, insurance, and logistics.
  • China Trade & Semis
    • No new tariffs yet, but elevated probabilities of policy actions in coming months—notably, US/China/EU meetings and export control debates are now tightly bound with AI and semiconductor company guidance.
  • Europe
    • ECB and BoE are near holds; not signaling imminent policy change. Eurozone's real growth remains soft—expectation for modest monetary support as energy costs rise.

2. Sector Implications

Energy, Commodities, & Transports

  • Oil Market
    • Severe supply chain risks: elevated realized and implied volatility. WTI is swinging ($62–$71), futures imply $90–$115 scenarios if conflict spirals or shipping blockades persist.
    • Second-order impacts: Refining, chemicals, container shipping rates, aviation (fuel, insurance).
  • Insurance
    • Direct risk to marine/transport insurers and catastrophe reinsurance. Major claims likely from shipping and infrastructure attacks.
    • Underwrites for Gulf region, Red Sea, and related areas will see rapid hardening.
  • Utilities & Permitting
    • No near-term movement, but data center and utility cost-protection discussions surfacing in policy.

Defense & Aerospace

  • Direct Beneficiaries
    • Defense primes (e.g., missile and naval systems) benefit from intensifying US and allied inventories and new deployment requirements. Suppliers may see capacity constraints if escalation persists.

Financials, Real Estate, & Housing

  • Banks & CRE
    • No acute liquidity stress—Fed index and spreads remain contained.
    • Mortgage rates stalled in high 6% range; housing activity showing resilience but is at risk if market volatility resumes.
    • Shutdown risk: payments and fee-dependent segments will experience some drag if DHS/other branches remain dysfunctional.
  • REITs
    • Risk a protracted shutdown triggers another leg down in tenants’ ability to pay and slows public CRE transactions through regulatory backlog.

Technology & AI

  • Semis/Cloud
    • US and China are converging on AI/semiconductor as state-level strategic competition—policy risk high for companies like NVDA and hyperscalers with China exposure.
    • AI infrastructure supply chain (especially for government contracts) now faces additional scrutiny: Pentagon warns on Anthropic AI use.
  • Big Tech
    • Social/AI application turf wars intensify. Policy focus broadening to encompass both content/platform and AI infrastructure regulation.

Consumer & Discretionary

  • Sentiment
    • No broad collapse in consumer confidence, but looming cost pressures if gasoline/energy prices remain high.
    • Retailers and restaurants should brace for higher COGS and near-term demand sensitivity if fuel/food prices spike on shipping/energy risk.

3. Company-Specific Themes

  • Anthropic (AI)
    • Removed from DoD systems. Likely to trigger broader review of AI contracts in defense and intelligence. Key second-order effect: opens up share for competitors (possibly MSFT/OpenAI, Google) and may spill over to private sector procurement.
  • Meta
    • Moltbook acquisition: expanding into AI-only social networks. Strategic aim appears to be dominance in “AI agent” discovery and interaction—peripherally bullish for AI cloud consumption, but regulatory and privacy friction likely.
  • Tesla
    • No near-term CEO risk. Auto sector: deliveries and margin focus may shift away from volume to mix and pricing if energy remains unsettled.
  • Energy Primes (Defense)
    • Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, Northrop, etc., post near-term bid for new contracts as MidEast tension persists—probable top-line upside over next several quarters.

4. Thematic & Alternative Assets

  • Gold & Precious Metals
    • Geopolitical risk and real rates support bullish thesis—price moves justified by actual reserves and policy signals. Expect further volatility around any IEA reserve move or escalations.
  • Crypto
    • Spot ETF flows (“positive/negative” near 50-50), crypto market remains headline-driven but generally more muted than in 2025.
    • Regulatory posture is tightening: SEC and CFTC expected to make rulings in Q2–Q3, adding risk to short-term price action in major tokens.
    • Whale actors (MicroStrategy, Bitmine) and country-level reserve strategies continue to drive narrative, but fundamental catalysts muted absent regulatory clarity.

5. Ongoing/Latent Risks

  1. US Federal/Agency Shutdown: Duration rising, real GDP impact grows as more agencies are affected and public–private transaction risk climbs. Regulatory backlog (housing, banks, pharma) and bottlenecks will be a growing headache.
  2. Iran/Red Sea/Strait Escalation: Energy/commodities are in the crosshairs, and any further disruption is unambiguously inflationary and likely risk-off for global equities.
  3. Labor & Corporate Bankruptcies: Tech remains at risk into mid-year, but no broad-based uptick in bankruptcies. Government and contractor layoffs likely delayed due to political logjam.
  4. Bond Market/Rate Volatility: Potential for negative convexity squeezes if CPI surprises or additional rate path uncertainty emerges from leadership changes.
  5. SCOTUS/Regulatory Rulings: Decisions around tariffs, environmental regulation, and tech antitrust will drive index and sector volatility.
