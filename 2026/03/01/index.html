Macro & Rates

Inflation & Growth

  • Inflation:

    • Core CPI (FRED, Jan): 332.79, trend is incremental. Core PCE (Dec): 127.92, also slow, but no recent sign of sharp reacceleration.
    • Market conveys above-2% CPI is sticky for 2026 (predictions: 2.4–2.8% mid-year, modest risk of >3.5% inflation, low-probability >4%).
    • Fiscal and supply shocks tied to Mideast escalation could upend near-term inflation assumptions; commodities (oil/gold) risk upward spikes.

  • Growth:

    • Labor market remains stable but showing modest cracks:
      • Unemployment rate (Jan): 4.3%, up modestly YoY.
      • Nonfarm payrolls (Jan): growth pattern is flat, not recessionary, but payroll gains/losses have little momentum.
      • Consumer sentiment (Jan): weak at 56.4, no v-shaped recovery.
      • GDP betting signals point to 2026 U.S. real GDP between 2–3.5% most likely, but probability of <1.5% or negative growth has increased marginally given recent exogenous shocks.

  • Central Bank:

    • Fed Funds currently in the 3.6–3.7% range and market expects at least one meaningful rate cut by end Q2/Q3 (multi-cut year).
    • Probability of four or more cuts in 2026 is real—largely a macro insurance bid. However, recession is not consensus.
    • Yield curve remains compressed, but positive (2s10s ~ +0.6%). NO sign of acute systemic U.S. funding stress.
    • If oil surges, the next CPI prints become extremely relevant for rate path—pace of rate cuts may stall if sustained.

  • U.S. sovereign: Debt is expected to hit or exceed $40T before 2027, ongoing concern of slow-motion loss of fiscal anchor.
  • Credit spreads: Despite war risk and defensive markets, Chicago Fed financial conditions remain "loose," supporting risk asset multiples for now.
  • Lingering tail risk: U.S. government shutdown risk not off table given repeated continuing resolution cycles. Any lapse could cause late Q2 volatility in Treasuries, agencies, and large contractors.
